# Indriyasthāna — Section on the Signs of Life and Death

*Source pages 528-566 of Sharma 2014, Vol. I.*

The Indriyasthāna ("section on the sense organs") collects the prognostic signs by which a physician reads the remaining span of life — the *ariṣṭa* (fatal signs) that precede death and the contrary auspicious signs that promise recovery. It contains 12 chapters.

## Chapters

1. [Varṇasvarīya](01-varna-svariya.md) — on complexion and voice (pp. 529-532)
2. [Puṣpitaka](02-pushpitaka.md) — on the flowered person (pp. 532-534)
3. [Parimarśanīya](03-parimarshaniya.md) — on palpable entities (pp. 535-537)
4. [Indriyānīka](04-indriyanika.md) — on sense organs (pp. 537-539)
5. [Pūrvarūpīya](05-purvarupiya.md) — on prodromal signs (pp. 540-543)
6. [Katamāniśarīrīya](06-katamanisaririya.md) — on physical abnormalities (pp. 544-546)
7. [Pannarūpīya](07-pannarupiya.md) — on shadow and lustre (pp. 546-549)
8. [Avākśirasīya](08-avakshirasiya.md) — on inverted image etc. (pp. 549-552)
9. [Yasyaśyāvanimittīya](09-yasyashyavanimittiya.md) — on blackish signs etc. (pp. 552-554)
10. [Sadyomaraṇīya](10-sadyomaraniya.md) — on signs of sudden death (pp. 554-556)
11. [Aṇujyotīya](11-anujyotiya.md) — on poor digestion etc. (pp. 556-559)
12. [Gomayachūrṇīya](12-gomayachurniya.md) — on cowdung-like powder etc. (pp. 559-566)
