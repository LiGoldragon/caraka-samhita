# Sūtrasthāna · Chapter 5 — Mātrāśitīya

*On quantity of food etc.*

> पञ्चमोऽध्यायः

> अथातो मात्राशितीयमध्यायं व्याख्यास्यामः ॥१॥

Now (I) shall expound the chapter on quantity of diet etc. **[1]**

> इति ह स्माह भगवानात्रेयः ॥२॥

As propounded by Lord Ātreya. **[2]**

> मात्राशी स्यात्, आहारमात्रा पुनरग्निबलापेक्षिणी ॥३॥

One should take food in (proper) quantity. This quantity of food depends on the power of digestion. **[3]**

> यावद्व्यवसनमतितानुद्वहतः प्रकृतिं यथाकालं जरां गच्छति तावद्स्य मात्राप्रमाणं वेदितव्यं भवति ॥४॥

Whatever quantity of food taken gets digested in time without disturbing the normalcy should be regarded as the measure of (proper) food. **[4]**

> तत्र शालिषष्टिकामुहलावकलापप्लेणशरशलशरभरम्भभरादीनामाहाराव्यास्यानि प्रकृतिलघून्यपि माथप्रयोगीणि भवन्ति ।
> तथा पिष्टशुकुलविकृतिलेतानपाडूपधूद्विकारकायचिनगुद्भाहाराण्यावि प्रकृतिगुरुयाणि माथाभ्यपयोगीष्विरमाक्ष्मीतु (५।।

Thus the articles of food such as śāli, ṣaṣṭika (rice), mudga (pulse), lāva, kapiñjala, eṇa, śaśa, śarabha, śambara (meats) etc. even though light by nature depend on quan- tity. Similarly, those such as preparations of flour, sugarcane, milk, sesamum, black gram, marshy and aquatic meats even though heavy in digestion by nature also depend on the quantity itself **[5]**

> न चैवमुक्ते द्रव्ये गुरुलाघवमकारणं मन्त्र्यते, लघूनि हि द्रव्याणि वाव्व्वाग्रुणबहुलानि भवन्ति,
> गुर्वान्युपार्पात्विगुणबहुलानि, तस्मात् स्वगुणादेव सिपृच्छित्वण्युदगबन्धाप्रजतावन्त्यम्ये बोध्येन्देऽपि सौहित्येपयुज्यानि,
> गुरूणि पुनर्नाज्गिशायुक्लमन्यथ-बाग्लासाम्न्यात् । आतगोत्तस्मात् दौषकले सौहित्येतेन तथिव्य व्याधामान्युक्ता; सैक्या
> चन्यभक्तमात्रेषिणी मात्रा ।।६।।

By this, it should not be confused that lightness and heaviness of (food) articles are quite inert. In fact, light articles are predominant in properties of vāyu and agni while others (heavy ones) are the same in those of pṛthivī and soma (ap). So, by dint of their properties, the light articles being stimulant of digestion, even taken upto the saturation point, produce little derangement. (On the contrary) the heavy articles becasue of dissimilarity, are not stimulant of digestion by nature and thus cause con- siderable derangment if taken upto the saturation point except in case (of persons having) physical exercise and (adequate) strength of agni (digestion). Hence, the quantity (of food) is related to agnibala (power of digestion). **[6]**

> न च नापेक्षते द्रव्यं; द्रव्यापेक्षया वा गुणसंसिद्ध्यै वा गुरूणामुपदिष्यते, लघूनामपि च नातिसौहित्य-
> मिष्टमग्निवर्गम् ॥७॥

It is not so that the article itself has no importance in this respect. (In fact) con- sidering the (property of the) article, the intake of heavy ones is advised as one- third or half of the saturation point and not excessive saturation even in case of light articles in order to maintain the proper (strength of) agni. **[7]**

> मात्रावद्व्यवहिताशितमनुगृह्णद्धि प्रकृतिं बलवर्णसुखायुषा योजयत्युपशेतारमबाधार्थाभिः ॥८॥

The food taken in proper quantity provides certainly strength, complexion and happy life to the person without disturbing normalcy. **[8]**

> भवति चात्र–

> गुरु भुक्तवान् लम्बाशयपूर्णगुह्यान् पृषकादीनि । न जातु भुक्तवान् खादेन्मात्रां खादेद् बुभुक्षितः ॥९॥

Here are (the verses)–

A person, after having taken food, should never eat heavy preprations of flour, rice and flattened rice. Even when hungry, one should take them in (proper) quantity. **[9]**

> सततगुरु शुष्कशाकानि बिसमूलानि च । नाभ्यसेच्छुष्कमांसं शूकरं धन्वमृगं च नाशीयेत् ॥१०॥
> कूर्चिकांश्च किलाटांश्च शुक्तं गव्यान्पतांसि च । एवं मत्स्यं कूर्बं कृष्णग्रामं च यावकम् ॥११॥

One should not take constantly dried meat, dried vegetable, lotus tubers and stem because of their heaviness. The meat of emaciated (animal) should not be taken. Similarly one should not take continuously kūrcikā and kilāṭa (milk products), pork, beef, buffalo meat, fish, curb, black gram and yāvaka. **[10-11]**

> षष्टिकशालिमुद्गांश्च सैन्धवान्लवणं घृतम् । आमलकानि धान्यं च यवान् क्षीरं घृतं मधु ॥१२॥

One should take as daily routine ṣaṣṭika, śāli (rice), mudga (pulse), saindhava (rock salt), āmalaka (fruits), barely, rain water, milk, ghee and honey. **[12]**

> नित्यं नियुक्तः स्वास्थ्यं धैर्ययुक्तं । अजातानां विकाराणामनुत्पादकरं च यत् ॥१३॥

One should take as a daily routine the articles which maintain health and prevent unborn disorders. **[13]**

> अत ऊर्ध्वं शरीरस्य कार्यमञ्जनतर्पकम् । स्वास्थ्यरक्षणभावैश्च गुणतः संग्रह्यते ॥१४॥

Hereafter the bodily daily routine like collyrium etc. will be described in relation to their role in maintaining health. **[14]**

> सौवीरमञ्जनं नित्यं हितमक्ष्णोर्विशेषतः । प्रयोजयेत पञ्चाहे अष्टाहे वा रसाञ्जनम् ॥१५॥
> चक्षूर्तेजोमयं तस्य विशेषाच्छ्लेष्मणो भयम् । तेनास्य संहरेच्छ्लेष्माणमादर्शतलवत्ततः ॥१६॥
> दिवा तत्प्रयोगान्नयनासुखप्राप्तिनाद्व्यसात् । विरिक्तं नेत्रमर्कस्य न सहेत् प्रभया दीप्तिम् ॥१७॥
> तस्माद्रसाञ्जनं रात्रौ स्वास्थ्ये श्रेयोऽभिपूज्यते ॥१७॥

The use of sauvīra añjana (collyrium) should be applied to eyes daily and rasāñjana once every five or eight days for stimulating

secretion. The vision is predominant in tejas and as such is specially susceptible to kapha. Hence the measures alleviating kapha are beneficial for clarifying the vision. A sharp collyrium should not be applied to eyes at day because the vision already weak due to evacuation is further depressed in the sun. Hence it is desirable certainly that the evacuative collyrium be applied only at night. **[15-17]**

> यथा हि कनकाद्यानां मलिनां विविधात्मनाम् ॥१८॥
> धौतानां निर्मला शुद्धिलेलोवलोऽसक्तादिधिः । एवं नेतेषु मर्त्यानामेतद्वानोेद्यावातिभिः ॥१९॥
> दृष्टिरैनीकुला भाति निर्मले नभसीन्दुवत् ।

As various types of metals like gold etc. when dirty become stainlessly clean after washing with oil, cloth, and hair (brush) etc., the vision in eyes of the mortal ones brightens undisturbed like moon in the clear sky by (application of) collyrium, eye drops etc. **[18-19]**

> हरेणुकां प्रियंगुं च पृष्ठीकां केशरं नखम् ।।२०।।
> ह्रीबेरं चन्दनं चर्च त्वगेलोप्लपद्मकम् । व्याधघकं मधुकं मांसी गुग्गुलुस्तगरुर्वयम् ।।२१।।
> न्यग्रोधोदुम्बरप्लक्षलोहप्रवकान्यं । शुभाः । वन्यं सर्जरसं सुरसं शैलेयं कमलोत्पलम् ।।२२।।
> श्रीवेष्टकं शल्लकीन्न शुष्कचन्दयनादि च । पिण्ड्या निसमैःक्षपयैर्वा तो वा सर्वं घरसर्विघाण् ।।२३।।
> अङ्गुष्टसन्मितां कुर्यादङ्गलायतसमाम् बिषकम् । शुष्कं निगच्छं ताँ वर्तिं धूमनेत्रादिषितं नरः ।।२४।।
> स्नेहैः साध्यभिसंयुक्तां पिबेद्ग्वाधायिष्कौ सुखाम् ।

Hareṇukā, priyaṅgu, pṛthvīkā, keśara, nakha, hrībera, candana, patra, twak, elā, uśīra, padmaka, dhyāmaka, madhuka, māṃsī, guggulu, aguru, śarkarā, bark of nyagrodha, udumbara, aśwattha, plakṣa and lodhra, vanya, sarjarasa, musta, śaileya, kamala, utpala, śrīveṣṭaka, śallakī and śhauneyaka–all these should be ground and pasted on a reed so as to make it a stick, barley-shaped, thumb-like and of eight fingers in length. The person should take this stick when dried and with free interior, put it on the smoking pipe and having added some fat and ignited should smoke as a daily routine for happiness. **[20-24]**

> वसायुतमधूच्छिष्टेमुंस्मिधुमुलिनीर्विभि: । १२१५।।
> वर्ति मधुरकैः: कृत्वा स्नेहैर्बद्धं धूममापबेतु ।

One should use unctuous smoking with stick made of useful drugs of sweet group added properly with fat, ghee and bee- wax. **[25]**

> श्वेता ज्योतिष्मती चैव हरितालं मनःशिला ।।१६।।
> गन्धाइगुर्प्रदशा धूपं धूर्विसरेचने ।

For evacuation of head, the smoking made of śwetā, jyotiṣmatī, orpiment, realgar and the aromatic substances (of agurvādya guṇa-see ci. 3.267) beginning with aguru and patra (leaving kustha and tagara) should be used. **[26]**

> गौरवं शिरसः शूलं पीनसार्धावभेदकी ।।२७।।
> कर्णाक्षिशूलं कासश्च हिक्काश्वासे गलग्रहः: । दन्तदौर्बल्यमाखावः क्रोत्रघ्राणाक्षिदोषजम् ।।२८।।

> पूतिघ्राणास्यगन्धश्च दन्तशूलमरोचकः । हनुमन्याग्रहः कण्डूः क्रिमयः पाण्डुता मुखे ॥२९॥
> श्लेष्मप्रसेकवैस्वर्यं गलशुण्डुक्गालेहिका । खालित्यं पिकरत्वं च केशानां पतनं तथा ॥३०॥
> स्रवथुइ्लान्तिता च बुद्धेर्मोहोऽल्पनिद्रता । धूमपानात नश्यन्ति प्रशान्त्यर्थं बलं भवति चासिकम् ॥३१॥
> शिरोरुहकपालानाम्गिन्दियाणां स्वरस्य च । न च तानि भवेयुर्यष्णि जायन्ते वातकाफतः ॥३२॥
> धूमबवक्शक्षपूयाणाम व्याख्यातः स्यु: शिरोगताः ।

Heaviness and pain in head, chronic rhinitis, hemicrania, earache, pain in eyes, cough, hiccup, dyspnoea, spasm in throat, weakness of teeth, discharge due to disor- ders of ear, nose and eyes, foetid smell from nose and mouth, toothache, anorexia, spasm in jaw and back neck, itching, worms, pale-face, excessive salivation, disor- ders of voice, tonsillitis, enlarged uvula, alopecia, grey hairs, hair falling, sneezing, excessive drowsiness, lack of functioning of intellect, excessive sleep–these disor- ders are alleviated by smoking. So, by dint of this, strength of hairs, skull, sense organs and voice increases. Moreover, the person used to oral smoking does not fall prey to dis- ease of the parts above jatru (collar bone) having predominance of vāta and kapha, however strong they are. **[27-32]**

> प्रयोगगधे तस्मात्तौ कालौ: संप्रकीर्तितौ ।।३३।।
> वातश्लेष्मसमुज्त्क्लेशः: कालेष्वेषु हि लक्ष्यते । स्नात्वा धुत्ता समुक्तिस्त्वा हुत्वा दन्ताशित्वं च ।।३४।।
> छवनाङ्कनिहानाते क्षायामये धुम्पो भवेत् । तथा वातकाकानां न भवन्पूर्ध्वजस्मुजः: ।।३५।।
> रोगास्तस्यम तु धेयाः: स्युःप्रयत्नाद्यैद्यनियश्चेत्येः ।

Eight times are prescribed for smoking as a daily routine because in these times the aggravation of vāta and kapha is noticed. A cautious person should smoke after bath, meals, vomiting, sneezing, tooth brushing, snuffing, use of collyrium and sleep. Thus diseases of the parts above jatru and predominant in vāta and Kapha do not arise. Smoking should be done thrice with three puffs each time. **[33-35]**

> परं द्विकालमन्यत्र स्याद्पु: कालेषु बुद्धिमान् ।।३६।।
> प्रयोगे, स्नैहिके लोके, वैरेचन त्रिभिस्तु; पिबेत् ।

During the times of day the wise should smoke twice as daily routine, once for unctuous and three or four times for evacuative effect. **[36]**

> हृत्कण्ठेन्द्रियसंशुद्धिलघुतपनरिसः: शमः ।।३७।।
> यथेरितारां दोषाणां सम्यग्वीतस्य लक्षणम् ।

Well cleaning of heart, throat and sense organs, lightness of head and pacification of aforesaid doṣas–these are the symptoms of proper smoking. **[37]**

> बाधिर्यमान्य मूर्च्छा रक्तपित्तं शिरोभ्रमम् ।।३८।।
> अकालो चातियीतो धूमः: कुर्यातप्रयवम् ।

Smoking used in excess and untimely causes complication such as deafness, blind- ness, dumbness, innate haemorrhage and vertigo. **[38]**

> तत्रेजे सर्पिषिः: पानं नावनाझ्जनतर्पणम् ।।३९॥

> स्नैहिकं धूमपाने दोषे वायुः; पित्तानुगो यदि । शीतं तु रक्ष्यिते स्याल्लेप्नोपिते विश्वकणम् ।।४०।।

In such cases, intake of ghee, snuff, collyrium and saturating is desirable. In case of complications due to smoking, these should be the unctuous if vāyu follows pitta, cold in raktapitta and roughening in kapha-pitta. **[39]**

> धूमयोग: सकृत्वर्थ्या धूमं घेषो विनिर्दिष्टा ।
> न रक्षौ न विषेणात्ते न स्नोजो वा गर्भिणीम् ।।४१।।
> न रक्खे दुखितं च हद्धं न च मद्ये नगो या विषै च ग्रसस्तनम् ।।४२।।
> न मुच्छार्चप्रभृत्यायु न बाधों नावि न क्षते । न मधक्ति मांद्यदेव च । लेहिनः परखच्छै: कीर्ति मधोऽपि न राउिडकानु भाति ।
> न शङ्खकेच न रौहिण्यां न मदात्यये च मेहे ।। पशु धूमकाले्षु नोहाद् चिन्ना साबि न तरि ।।४५।।
> रोगास्तस्य प्रवर्तन्ते दारुणा धूमविभ्रमात् ।

Now I shall say about person smoking is contra-indicated. One should not smoke after purgation, enema and bleeding, one afflicted with poison, anxiety, pregnant, in case of fatigue, narcosis, āma, pitta, vigil, fainting giddiness, thirst, emaciation, injury; after taking wine, milk, fatty substances and honey, food with curd; in conditions of roughness, anger, dryness of palate, defects of vision, head injury, Śaṅkhaka, rohinī, prameha and alcoholism. One who smoke in these condi- tions and untimely due to carelessness, the disorders aggravate severly due to com- plication of smoking. **[41-45]**

> धूमयोगः: पिबेद्योने शिरोप्राणागसंश्रया । १४६।।
> प्राणेनायम्य मूर्ख्य सुखं प्राणयो वषेत् । आत्स्येन धूमकवलमन्तिः पिबन् धायेंन योजभेत् ।।४७।।
> प्रतिलोमो गतो द्राक्ष्या धूमो हिर्स्यादि चक्षुषी ।

One fit for smoking should smoke through nostrils in disorders located in head, nose and eyes, and through mouth in those of throat. While smoking through nostrils, one should emit through mouth but while smoking through mouth, one should not emit through nostrils because the smoke traversing reversely damages the eyes imme- diately. **[46-47]**

> ऋज्वज्जवहृतस्वेताः: सुपविष्टोसर्गप्रयुक्तः ।।४८।।
> पिबेन्निष्ट पिधायैका नासया धूमयात्मवान् ।

One should smoke through one nostril closing the other one three rounds, thrice in each round, cautiously while sitting comfortably with straight body and eyes and concentrated mind. **[48]**

> चतुर्विंशतिकं नैव स्त्राङ्गुलीभिर्विंसने ।।४९।।
> द्वाविंशत्युलं स्नेहे प्रयोगेऽष्ठद्शात्मिते ।

The smoking pipe should be, by own fingers, of twenty four fingers length in evacuation, of thirty two fingers in snuff and of eighteen fingers in routine smoking. **[49]**

> ऋजु किकोचिकारहितं कोलास्थ्यमधुनिर्गतम् ।।५०।।
> वस्तिनेत्रसमद्मव्यं धूमनेत्रं प्रशस्यते ।

Smoking pipe which is straight, having three pouches and nozzle of the measure of jujube seed, made of material similiar to that for enema pipe is recom- mended. **[50]**

> दुर्गाधिनिर्गः : पर्वैव्छौ नाडीमप्कर्त्कत् ।।५१।।
> नेत्रेय बाघते धूमो मांसशल्लिनिवेषितः ।

Smoke coming out from distance, intervened by nodes and diluted by pipe chan- nel and used in proper dose and time does not damage the sense organs. **[51]**

> यदा कोष्ठं कफ इश्च शिरश्च लघुतां व्रजेत् ।
> कफरा कुस्तं वातंपच्छ्न्यूज् ये । अविष्यभक्ि: स्वरो रस्य कण्ठचुं सक्तकौ भवेत् ॥५२॥
> सितिंमतो मसराकीतंमौ धूमादिशतो । लागु मुर्षा च कष्टस्य वक्षो गहहोवक्षतेः ॥५३॥
> इन्द्रियाणसौरितेषु सुखवाभपति चाय्यवम् । निद्रालपातेः: सुखं स्यात् सम्यग्धीतस्य लिंक्षणम् ॥५४॥

Smoking is said as well-used when chest, throat and head attain lightness and kapha is diluted.

The same is said as ill-used if voice is not clear, throat is full of kapha and head is cold heavy.

In case of excessive smoking, palate, head and throat dry up with burning sensation, the person gets thirsty and fainted, bleeding is increased, there is excessive giddiness and fainting and dysfunctioning of sense organs. **[52-53]**

> तत्र साब्यपूर्तोत्तं स कालेषु विवे्वैं हरेन् ।।५४।।
> प्रायुगर्गप्रसदानेउ गौर्तनो नभसन्यन्सेतु । न तस्य बहुष्णे प्राण न सखुष्यत्ते । ह्ेषमृत्षी य वा पुनः ।।५४।।
> न तस्य बाह्यं प्राप्य: क्ले पाटिपतिप्लिप्तेवानि तस्य । अवगन्धाकेशस्यान्नः: सरिवा; शिरेरामेतेते ।।५५।।
> नेवैदाव्वाणंर्द्वेन्यं बलं शीर्षस्य साधिष्ये । न तस्य रोगः: सहसा प्रभवन्त्यूवेर्जग्रु जतजा ।।५६।।
> सर्वेशान्राणी वैरस्य: बलं यस्थेति चाधिकम् । न तस्य रोगाः: सहसा प्रभवन्त्यूर्धजत्रुजाः ।।५६।।
> जीर्णक्षप्ीनमाद्यपि सुलक्ष्म का लखते बलम् ।

Every year one should use anutaila (through snuff) thrice a day in early rains, autumn and spring when the sky is free from clouds.

One who practises snuffing as prescribed and in time, his vision, smell and hearing are not affected, his hairs, beard and mustache do not become white or grey, hairs do not fall rather they grow abundantly. Stiffness in side neck, headache, facial paralysis, lockjaw, chronic rhinitis, migraine and head tremors are alleviated. Veins, joints, ligaments and tendons of skull attain greater strength on saturation through snuffing. Face becomes cheerful and well-developed, voice melodius, stable and grave. Freedom from defects and increased strength are bestowed upon all sense-organs. He is not attacked suddenly by disorders of parts above jatru and

even in advanced years, old age does not find strength in his best organ, the head (head). **[56-62]**

> चन्दनागुरु पत्र दारूशित्वङ्मधुकं बलाम् ।।६३।।
> प्रपीण्डरीकं सूक्ष्मेलां विडङ्गं विल्वमुत्पलम् । ह्रीबेरमर्पे बर्णं त्वगुलसुरां सारिवां शिरसम् ।।६४।।
> जीवन्तिं वृद्धिकौ पर्णींच सुरदारू शताबरी । सुष्क्ष्णप्रमाणबलादम्यान शततः कधासानन्त्यः: ।।६५।।
> पक्रियापवेकरलानी मांडेक्टे विमलौधस्मीः । तेलालांशोधा पाक्क कावसथामेवा । १६६।।
> तेन तैत सुलक्षयोद्दं दशकर्णाबलेयतेव । तैलायाशन्यापोयेर्छ दशामधिविर्द्यिते । ।।६६।।
> दधाहोयोऽमृतर्णं स्वर्णेंज्ज्ञीनिलिश्द्दा: । अस्थानात्रितिवृतेतेषुजाङ्दात्रीती खेलेसेजनिनिनांः । १६८।।
> निवातदासख्वासारी हितानी निर्मलस्क्विमत्ते: । व्याशाय्रस्त्राणविधः: दोषांनाचौतेयरेरि न्यानाद्य । ।।६९।।
> निवाताय्रस्त्राणविधः: दोषणानाचौतेयरेरि खेलेलीमीची । ।।७७।।
> प्रयुक्तानां यथाकालं यदिष्टं गुणभोषयते ।

Candana, aguru, patra, dāruharidrā (bark), madhuka, balā, prapauṇḍarīka, sūkṣma elā, viḍaṅga, bilwa, utpala, hrībera, karīra, vaca, tagara, uśīra, sārivā, kaivarta mustā, jīvantī, devadāru, śatāvarī, hareṇu, bṛhatī, śālaparṇī, padmakeśara–these drugs should be boiled in hundred times pure rain water and the remaining decoction which is ten times of oil should be taken. With this decoction the oil should be cooked ten times adding equal quantity of goat's milk in the tenth cooking. This is the method (of preparation) of anutaila to be used as snuff. This oil should be used in dose of half pala (20 ml.). After oleating and fomenting head parts, the snuff should be taken thrice with a cotton swab on every third day for a week. During this period, the person should keep himself in wind-free and warm place, should take whole-some food and having control on sense organs. Thus using this oil, destroyer of tridoṣa and strength-giving to sense organs, in proper time one attains the aforesaid benefits. **[63-70]**

> आयोधितायं द्वौ कालौ कषायकटुतिलकञ्च ।।७०।।
> भवेत्वेव्ययायर्य दन्तधावनायत्वरीवम् । निजर्वे नर्म वैरस्यं दन्तविदर्शोपनिक्त्वयम् ।।७१।।
> निष्कृष्य रुचिमायेम साधो दन्तविराधिनम् । कराङ्कर्वीरिकर्वरच्वाकीजनासानाः ॥७२।।
> शस्यने दन्तपर्ये वै चान्ध्यमेष्मव । मसूरकालकिक स्याद ।
> विड्जर्विलस्यरूसत्पूर्णीम्जपर्कृनोवपुच्छं थरुज्ञीरिमे । ।७३।।
> डिजमुनिमनजीवमाहातेरुस्यल्पाक्तुदल्यस्य इतभुष्रेलाकेंमस्जवत् । ।७४।।
> विविधोन्यसभार्रिक हित प्रयितिच्छद्वा । इल्लेस्मनातरिक्तस्नेनिद्ध्यानं प्रलापेति ।। ७७५।।
> प्रयुक्तजने व्यत्य हिवनीनन्धुं मैनिनिलिलयोदम् ।

Candana, aguru, pṛthvākā, keśara, nakha, hrībera, candana, patra, twak, elā, uśīra, padmaka, dhyāmaka, madhūka, māṃsī, kaivarta musta, sārivā, śālaparṇī, jīvantī, pṛśniparṇī, devadāru, śatāvarī, hareṇu, bṛhatī, kaṇṭakārī, rāsnā, padmakeśara–these drugs should be boiled in hundred times pure rain water and the remaining decoction which is ten times of oil should be used. With this decoction the oil should be cooked ten times adding equal quantity of goat's milk in the tenth cooking. This is the method (of preparation) of anutaila to be used as snuff. This oil should be used in dose of half pala (20 ml.). After oleating and fomenting head parts, the snuff should be taken thrice with a cotton swab on every third day for a week. During this period, the person should keep himself in wind-free and warm place, should take whole-some food and having sense control on sense organs. Thus using this oil, destroyer of tridoṣa and strength-giving to sense organs, in proper time one attains the aforesaid benefits. **[63-70]**

> आद्यातिः श्रेष्ठा कौ कलौ कषायकटुकिक्तानम् ।।७०।।
> भवेद्वै यष्टिसमधाँ: सुदन्तारस्य बलागम् । हरितं वर्जयदं तै रसदान्भ्यास्ततविटदि ।।७१।।
> द्विरर्दन व्वस्थथा: परं समृद्धि संध्दकदिभुद्धं ।

One should use astringent, pungent and bitter teeth-cleaning (twigs having made it into a brush) by crushing its top end, twice a day without injuring the gums. Teeth- cleaning removes (foul) smell, tastelessness and taking out dirt coated on tongue, teeth and mouth brings relish immediately.

Karañja, karavīra, arka, jātī, arjuna, asana and several similar plants are recommended for tooth brush.

Tongue-scrapers should be made of gold, silver, copper, tin and brass and should be non-sharp and curved.

The dirt which is collected at the root of the tongue and which creates obstruc- tion in respiration produces foul smell, hence one should scrape tongue. **[71-75]**

> धार्यौत्प्लस्येव वक्षासन्वितिना वस्तिमुखैण ।।७६।।
> जातीफलमकुपूरलं लवणं च । कक्षोलस्य फलं चय ताम्बूलस्य श्रृगं तथा । तथा कर्पूरनिर्यासं सुक्ष्मैलायाः फलानि च ।।७७।।

One desiring (taste), relish and fragrance should keep in mouth the fruits of jātī, katuka, pūga and lavaṅga; kakkola (fruit), auspicious leaves of betel, exudate of cam- phor and fruits of smaller cardamom. **[76-77]**

> हन्वोर्बलं स्वरसकं वदनोपचयः: परः । स्वाद् घरं च रसज्ञानमे रुचिस्य ।।७८।।
> न चास्य कण्ठशोषः: स्यान्कान्शायैद्ये न च दत्य ोः । हर्यं यापति चास्त्रत्य वज्जद्विखरा भवन्तित ।।७९।।
> न शुष्यन्त न चामचारं ह्मन्वचोर्षन्चलति वदनम् । परनाषि हार्द्रेतान पल्लशारीतेलीत्तोपघाट्सब्देसाम ।।८०।।

Use of oil gargle increases strength, development of face, taste, relief from dryness of throat, there is no fear of lip-cracking, teeth are not affected with caries rather they become firm-rooted. They (teeth) are not painful nor are they oversensi- tive on sour-taking, they become able to chew even the hardest food items. **[78-80]**

> नित्यं स्नेहाद्विणः: शिरःशूलं न जायते । न खालित्यं न पालित्यं न केशाः: प्रपतन्ति च ।।८१।।
> बलं शिरःकपालानामियैदवं कपालम् । पालिन्द्री गाण मुखं चतुरुरसान् ।।८२।।
> इन्द्रियाणि प्रसीदन्ति सुलनाश्रवत च चरण्या । नियादभारस्वण पलेमेचरवमागवात्तः ।।८३।।

One who smears his head with unctuous substance daily does not suffer from headache, alopecia, greying of hair, nor do his hairs fall. By applying oil on head regularly, strength of skull-parts increases particularly, hairs become firm-rooted, long and black, sense organs become cheerful and the well-developed, voice melodius, sound sleep and happiness. **[81-83]**

> न कर्णरोगा वातोत्था न मन्यास्तम्भः: । न दोष्टुतिभायाषिष्टः स्वस्त्यान् कर्णैतर्पमगारत् ।।८४।।

By saturating ears with oil daily, ear diseases due to vāta, stiffness of backneck and jaws, hard hearing and deafness do no occur. **[84]**

> स्नेहाभ्यञ्जना कुष्माङ्कयं स्नेहवन्नित्य् । भ्यष्नुनुग्रहत्वाद् दृढं क्लेशसहत्तं बा । क्लेशसहत्ता यथा ।।८५।।
> तथा शरीरमब्यस्रोभ्य सुलवकं च जायने । प्रशारम्भाणायायारि क्लेशव्यायामसंसहम् ।।८६।।

As a pitcher by moistening with oil, skin by pressing with oil and axis (of a cart) by using lubricant become strong and jerk-resistant, so by oil massage the body becomes firm, smooth-skinned, free from disturbances of vāta and tolerant of exer- tions and exercise. **[85-86]**

> स्पर्शनेन्द्रियं वायुः: स्पर्शने च त्वगाश्रितम् । त्वच्यश्च परमभ्यस्पादास्तमेतेरैम् ।।८७।।

Vāyu is predominant in tactile sense organ which again is located in skin, oil massage is the most beneficial for skin, hence one should use it regularly. **[87]**

> न सातपाताभिहतं गात्रमभ्यङ्गसेविनः । विकारं भजते पञ्चयं बलयापि वा कृषिता ॥८८॥
> सुप्रसादिषितादृद्धा बलवान् प्रियदर्शनः । त्वचाभ्यङ्गनित्यसारस्रौऽत्पन्नर एव च ॥८९॥

The body of one who uses oil massage regularly does not become affected much even if subjected to accidental injuries, or strenuous work. By using oil massage daily, a person is endowed with pleasant touch, trimmed body parts and becomes strong, charming and least affected by old age. **[88-89]**

> खरत्वं स्तब्धता रौक्ष्यं श्रमः: सुप्तिश्च पादयोः । सद्य एवोपशाम्यन्ति पादाभ्यञ्ज्निषेवणात् ॥९०॥
> जायते सौकुमार्य च बलं स्थैर्यं च पादयोः । दृष्टिः प्रसादं लभते मारुतश्वोपशाम्यति ॥९१॥
> न च स्याद् गृध्रसीजातः: पादयोः: स्फुटनं न च । न सिरास्नायुसंकोचः: पादाभ्यङ्गे न पादयोः ॥९२॥

By massaging of oil in feet, coarseness, stiffness, roughness, fatigue and numbness of feet are alleviated in no time. Further, delicacy, strength and firmness in feet, clarity of vision are attained and vāta is pacified. By massage of oil in feet, there is no occurence of sciatica, craking of sole and constriction of blood vessels and ligaments. **[90-92]**

> दौर्गन्ध्यं गौरवं तन्द्रां कण्डू मलमरोचकम् । स्वेदबीभत्सतां हन्ति शरीरपरिमार्जनम् ॥९३॥

Rubbing over the body alleviates foul smell, heaviness, drowsiness, itching, dirt, anorexia and vulgar appearance of sweat. **[93]**

> पवित्रं वृष्यमायुष्यं स्नानमूर्जबलप्रदम् । शरीरजरक्लमस्वर्गन्धहरमग्निदीनकम् पारम् ॥९४॥

Bathing is purifying, aphrodisiac, life-promoting, destroyer of fatigue, sweat and dirt, resuscitative and a good promoter of ojas. **[94]**

> कार्यं वशस्त्रमायुष्यमलक्ष्मीत्नं प्रहर्षणम् । श्रीमत् पवित्रं शस्तं निर्मलाहारयारणम् ॥९५॥

Wearing clean clothes enhances charm, fame, life span; removes inaus- piciousness, produces pleasure, auspiciousness and eligibility for a congregation. **[95]**

> वृष्यं सौगन्ध्यमायुष्यं कान्यं पुष्टिबलप्रदम् । सौमनस्यमलक्ष्मीघ्नं गन्धमात्यनिषेवणम् ॥९६॥

Use of fragrance and garlands is aphrodisiac, produces good smell, longevity, charm, nourishment and strength, pleasing manners and destroys inauspicious- ness. **[96]**

> धन्यं मङ्गल्यमायुष्यं श्रीमद्व्यासनसूदनम् । हर्षणं कान्यमोजस्यं रत्नाभरणधारणम् ॥९७॥

Wearing of jems and ornaments promotes, wealth, auspiciousness, longevity, prosperity; destroys calamity, enhances happiness, charms and ojas. **[97]**

> मेधां पवित्रमायुष्यमलक्ष्मीघ्नं सनैत्रणुवभर यत् । पादयोर्मलमार्गाणां शौचाधानमभीक्ष्णशः ॥९८॥

Cleaning of feet and excretory orifices frequently promots intelligence, purity, longevity, and destroys inauspiciousness and dirt. **[98]**

> पौंश्चिकं वृष्यमायुष्यं शुचि स्पर्शेन्द्रिय्यन् । केशश्मश्रुनखादीनां कल्पनं संप्रसाधनम् ॥९९॥

Cutting of hair, beard, moustaches, nail etc. and hair-dressing is nutritive, aph- rodisiac, life-promoter, and provides cleanliness and beautification. **[99]**

> चक्षुष्यं स्पर्शनहितं पादयोर्ब्वसनापहम् । बल्यं परक्रमसुखं वृष्यं पादत्राणधारणम् ॥१००॥

Use of foot wears is beneficial for eye-sight and tactile sense-organ, is destro- yer of calamity to feet and promotes strength, ease in display of energy and libido. **[100]**

> विषे: प्रशमनं बल्यं गुप्त्यावरणसाधकम् । पर्यापिःचरैश्मानुष्छं छत्रारणमुच्यते ॥१०१॥

Use of umbrella alleviates natural calamities, provides strength, protection, cov- ering and well being and guards against the sun, wind, dust and rains. **[101]**

> स्खलनं: संप्रतिष्ठानं स्त्युष्टिं च निसूदनम् । अवज्ञभ्यमायुष्यं भवनं दण्डधारणम् ॥१०२॥

Use of stick supports a slipping person, averts enemies, gives strength and lon- gevity and destroys fears. **[102]**

> नगरी नगरस्येव रथस्येव रथी यथा । स्वशरीरस्य मेधावी कृत्यव्यवहितो भवेत् ॥१०३॥

As a civic is cautious in the duties of the city and a charioteer in those of the chariot, a wise person should be cautious in duties relating to his own body. **[103]**

> भवति चात्र–
> वृत्त्युपायान्विशेषेत न स्मृतिर्मविरोधिनः । शुभमन्वष्ठयन्न चैव सुखमेव समुनुते ॥१०४॥

Here is said–

One should take up those means of livelihood which are not contradictory to dharma (social and religious ethics). Likewise, he should pursue life of peace and study. Thus he enjoys happiness. **[104]**

> तत्र श्लोकाः:–
> मात्रा द्रव्याणि मात्रां च लक्षिधां गुरुलाघवम् । द्रव्याणां गहितोऽेत्यभ्यसो येषो, येषो च शरस्वं ॥१०५॥
> अङ्गस्वै गृयान्विहितं त्रिविध वर्तिकल्पना । धूमपत्नमुपाः कालः; पानमात्रा च यस्य यत् ॥१०६॥
> व्यापनिषिद्धं वैषण्य धूमो येषां विगहितिः । येयो यदा वन्यो च नैतं यच्छ च चाह्विषम् ॥१०७॥
> नस्यकर्मगुणा नस्तःकार्य यच्च यदा । भङ्गवेधानयनं येषां चक्षुःस्वगुणं च यत् ॥१०८॥
> गण्डूकरंणिकयुनिगुणामैत्रैले न्यञ्जनगुणाः । इंद्नस्मे अयसरसंलमुभनायूयाभ्यो च यल ॥१०९॥
> कर्णेष्वै तद्ध्यु पादायोअस्य र्द्धानम्बः । स्नानं बालंसिं श्वुरं च सौगन्ध्यं सस्र्वार्णाङ्कात् ॥११०॥
> शोचे संहरणं लोहन्नां पादचरण्यवारणम् । गुणा मात्राशितीयेऽस्मिन्यासथा दण्डधारणाम्वस्ति ॥१११॥
