# Caraka Samhita — Selected Quotes

Extracted from *Caraka Samhita*, trans. Priya Vrat Sharma (Chaukhambha
Orientalia, 2014).


## On the Nature of Ayurveda

> "Ayurveda is that which deals with good, bad, happy and unhappy life, its
> promoters and non-promoters, measure and nature."
> — C.Su. 1.41

> "In the living beings, non-violence is the best among the life-promoting
> factors, prowess is the best among the strength-promoting ones, learning is
> the best among the bulk-promoting ones, control on sense organs is the best
> among the happiness-promoting ones, knowledge of reality is the best among
> the pleasure-promoting ones and celibacy is the best among the paths."
> — C.Su. 30.15

> "Ayurveda is eternal. It has not been composed by any human being since it is
> not a composition and hence has no beginning."
> — C.Su. 30.27


## On Life, Health and Disease

> "Disease-free condition is the best source of virtue, wealth, gratification
> and emancipation while diseases are destroyers of this source, welfare and
> life itself."
> — C.Su. 1.15–17

> "Mind, self and body — these three make a tripod on which the living world
> stands."
> — C.Su. 1.46–47

> "Abnormality is disequilibrium of dhatus and their equilibrium is normalcy.
> Health is known as happiness while disorder is unhappiness."
> — C.Su. 9.4

> "The happy life is that wherein there is no disturbance of dharma, artha,
> kama and moksa, while the unhappy life is that which is full of pains and
> vices."
> — C.Su. 30.22

> "The object of Ayurveda is to protect health of the healthy and to alleviate
> disorders in the diseased."
> — C.Su. 30.26


## On Food and Diet

> "Only the use of wholesome food promotes the growth of person and that of
> unwholesome one is the cause of disorders."
> — C.Su. 25.31

> "The food which maintains the balanced dhatus in normalcy and restores the
> equilibrium in mal-balanced ones, should be taken as wholesome; otherwise
> unwholesome."
> — C.Su. 25.33

> "On agni depend strength, health, life-span and vital breath. Agni burns with
> the fuel of food and drinks; otherwise it is impaired."
> — C.Su. 27.342

> "One taking wholesome food with controlled self lives healthy for one thousand
> and thirty-six nights (100 years) liked by the good men."
> — C.Su. 27.348

> "The six rasas in the mouth are: madhura, amla, lavana, katu, tikta and
> kasaya. These are produced from earth and water but differ in potencies
> because of the predominance of remaining elements."
> — C.Su. 26.40

> "Excessive use of bitter leads to depletion of all dhatus through its drying
> up action; of pungent leads to diminution of semen and muscle; of astringent
> leads to constipation, obstruction of channels and wasting."
> — C.Su. 26.43–45


## On Ghee

> "Cow ghee promotes memory, intelligence, agni, semen, ojas, kapha and medas;
> alleviates vata, pitta, poison, insanity, phthisis, inauspiciousness and
> fever. It is the best of all fats, is cold, madhura rasa, madhura vipaka,
> has thousand potentialities and so, if used properly according to prescribed
> methods, exerts thousand types of action."
> — C.Su. 27.231–233

> "Ghee is the best one for those desirous of intelligence, lustre of
> complexion, good voice, longevity, semen, happiness, tenderness of body and
> strength."
> — C.Su. 27.234


## On Cow Milk

> "The cow-milk has got these ten properties — sweet, cold, soft, unctuous,
> viscous, smooth, slimy, heavy, dull and clear. Thus it increases ojas having
> the same properties due to similarity. Hence cow milk has been said as the
> best one among vitalisers and as rasayana."
> — C.Su. 27.217

> "Cow milk alleviates vata and pitta. Taken at night it alleviates disorders of
> the heart, promotes eye-sight, is aphrodisiac and rasayana and bestows the
> effects of nectar in old age."
> — C.Su. 27.218–219


## On Water

> "Rain water is the most purified water and, as regards quality, is the best
> one. It has the properties of sky being similar, and is beneficial for all
> living beings."
> — C.Su. 27.200

> "The water, heated with the sunrays during day and cooled with moonrays
> during night, cooked by time, free from defects and detoxicated by agastya,
> is known as 'hamsodaka' which is obtained during autumn and is clean and
> pure. This water is beneficial like nectar if used in bath, drink and
> plunging."
> — C.Su. 6.41–48


## On Honey

> "Honey, if heated or taken by a person suffering from heat, becomes fatal due
> to its association with poisons."
> — C.Su. 27.245–246

> "There is nothing so severe as madhvama (ama caused by honey) as, because of
> its conflicting treatment, it immediately kills the person like poison."
> — C.Su. 27.247–248

> "Old honey is scraping, light, drying up and constipating; fresh honey is
> unctuous, nourishing and laxative."
> — C.Su. 27.244


## On Ojas

> "The substance of white or red, slightly yellowish colour which resides in
> heart is known as ojas. The person dies if it is destroyed. In the body of
> living beings the ojas is produced first. This has the colour of ghee, taste
> of honey and smell of fried paddy."
> — C.Su. 17.73–75

> "Ojas is the first thing produced in the body of a living being. The heart is
> the seat of consciousness. The person dies when ojas in the heart is
> destroyed."
> — C.Su. 30.4–5

> "Diminution of ojas is caused by anger, grief, anxiety, exertion, fasting,
> excessive activity, aggravated vata and so on."
> — C.Su. 17.78


## On the Tridosa

> "Vata, pitta and kapha in their normal state support the body and in their
> abnormal state, afflict it."
> — C.Su. 18.48

> "Vata, pitta and kapha — these three are like supporting columns of the body.
> They support it when in equilibrium and destroy it when disturbed."
> — C.Su. 12.12

> "Normal functions of vata are: enthusiasm, inspiration and expiration, proper
> movement of dosas, maintenance of dhatus in their places and initiation of
> all activities."
> — C.Su. 18.49

> "Normal functions of pitta are: vision, digestion, heat, hunger, thirst,
> lustre, intellect, courage, softness of body and cheerfulness."
> — C.Su. 18.50

> "Normal functions of kapha are: stability, lubrication, compactness of
> joints, forbearance, absence of greed and unctuousness of body."
> — C.Su. 18.51


## On Drugs and Medicine

> "A drug, if unknown, is fatal like poison, weapon, fire and thunderbolt
> while, if known, is like nectar."
> — C.Su. 1.124–125

> "A sharp poison also becomes the best drug by proper administration; on the
> contrary, even the best drug is reduced to sharp poison, if administered
> badly."
> — C.Su. 1.126

> "On this basis there is no substance in the universe which cannot be used as
> drug, on the condition that they are used rationally and with a definite
> objective."
> — C.Su. 26.12


## On the Physician

> "There are two types of physicians — one the promoters of vital breath and
> destroyers of diseases, and the other promoters of diseases and destroyers
> of vital breath."
> — C.Su. 29.5

> "Friendliness and compassion towards the diseased, interest in the amenable
> and indifference to those who are moving towards end — this is the fourfold
> attitude of physician."
> — C.Su. 9.26

> "The quack physician has got merely the outward appearance of a physician —
> he harms the people, like a fire covered with ashes."
> — C.Su. 29.12


## On Conduct and the Natural Urges

> "Perverted, negative and excessive use of time, intellect and sense objects is
> the threefold cause of both psychic and somatic disorders."
> — C.Su. 1.54

> "The wise should not suppress the impending urges of urine, faeces, semen,
> flatus, vomiting, sneezing, eructation, yawning, hunger, thirst, tears,
> sleep and breathing after exertion."
> — C.Su. 7.3–4

> "One desirous of well-being here and hereafter should hold up the urges of
> evil ventures relating to thought, speech and action."
> — C.Su. 7.26

> "He who is not addicted to sense objects, and is not averse to them, who takes
> food with due consideration, who has control over his sense organs and mind,
> who has no ego and is free from attachment and hatred — he is capable of
> conquering the diseases."
> — C.Su. 8.17–18


## On Samanya and Visesa

> "Samanya (similarity) is the cause of increase and visesa (dissimilarity) is
> the cause of decrease. This is the principle which operates in the universe
> among all the objects whether moving or static."
> — C.Su. 1.44–45


## On the Three Desires

> "Life, wealth and the other world — these are the three desires which are
> regarded as the basis of the world. A man in whom these are maintained is
> regarded as happy."
> — C.Su. 11.3–4
