# Sūtrasthāna · Chapter 2 — Apāmārga-taṇḍulīya

*On seeds of apāmārga etc.*

> द्वितीयोऽध्यायः

> अथातोऽपामार्गतण्डुलीयमध्यायं व्याख्यास्यामः ॥१॥

Now (I) shall expound the chapter on seeds of apāmārga etc. **[1]**

> इति ह स्माह भगवानात्रेयः ॥२॥

As propounded by Lord Ātreya. **[2]**

> अपामार्गस्य बीजानि पिप्पलीर्मरिचानि च । विडङ्गान्यथ शिग्रूणि सर्षपांस्तुम्बुरूणि च ॥३॥
> अजगन्धी चाजाजी च पीलुमूलं हरेणुकाम् । पृथ्वीकां सरलां कुष्ठं कुठेरकफणिज्जकौ ॥४॥
> शिरीषबीजं लशुनं हरिद्रे लशषैव च । पुष्पाणि मदयन्त्याश्च श्वेतायाः ज्योतिष्मत्याश्च ॥५॥
> तिरीटकान्यनन्ताया कसतुम्भस्य चालजीम् । किलिमकाभरि(?)एतानि धान्यचूर्णैः स्रोतसि ॥६॥
> गोरसे पित्तमेदोन्ने ट्रवत्त्यूष्मंणां प्रशान्तकः । शूलादरगुल्मो तत्संयो त्तत्र्य वेधेवीहीसक्तु च ॥७॥

Seeds of apāmārga; fruits of pippalī, marica, viḍaṅga, śigru, sarṣapa and tumburu, ajgandhā, ajājī, pīlu, harenuka, Pṛthvīkā, saralā, *kuṣṭha*, kuṭheraka, phañijjaka, śirīṣa (seeds), laśuna, haridrā (two types), buds of madayantī, śvetā, and śuṇṭhī are used for evacuation of head (*śirovirecana*), to be used in inflammatory disorders of *dravya* (of *doṣas*), *āmāśaya* without producing any harmful effect on the body. **[7-8]**

> त्रिवृतां त्रिफलां दन्तीं नीलिनीं सप्तलां व्यचां कम्पिल्लकं, गवाक्षीं, क्षीरिणीं,
> उदकीर्यकां पीलु, आरग्वधं, द्राक्षां, द्रवन्तीं च निचुल

*Trivṛt, triphalā, dantī, nīlinī, saptalā, vyacā, kampillaka, gavākṣī, kṣīriṇī, udakīryakā, pīlu, āragvadha, drākṣā, dravantī* and *nicula* — these drugs may be used for purgation in *doṣa* located in *pakvāśaya* (colon). **[9-10]**

> पाटलां चाग्निमन्थं च बिल्वं श्योनाकमेव च । काश्मर्यं शालपर्णीं च पृष्णिपर्णीं ॥११॥
> बलं बृहतीं बृहतीमेव सनुवर्नवा । यवान्, कुलत्थान् कोलानि गुडूचीं मदनानि च ॥१२॥
> पलाशं काशंणी चैव स्नेहलं लवणानि च । उदवर्ते विबन्धेषु गुह्मामादेशमामातनेषु ॥१३॥
> अन एवौषधगणात् संकल्प्यमनुपादरम् । पाङ्गन्यासेन्द्रि विधेषुः स्वाद्याः पाङ्गकर्मिक ॥१४॥

*Pāṭalā, agnimantha, bilva, śyonāka, kāśmarya, śālaparṇī, pṛśniparṇī, kaṇṭakārī, balā, gokṣura, bṛhatī, eraṇḍa, punarnavā, yava, kulattha, kola, guḍūcī, madana, palāśa, kaṭtṛṇa*, fats and salts — these alongwith *lavaṇa* are used for non-unctuous enema in *udāvarta* and constipation. With this very group of drugs may be prepared unctuous enema for alleviation of vāta. Thus *pañcakarma* (five measures) is said in brief. **[11-14]**

> तानूचर्वमचातोदेषणां स्नेहस्वेदोपपादये: । पञ्च कर्माणि कुर्वीत मात्राकालो विचारयन् ॥१५॥

These five measures may be applied to those having presence of *doṣas* after performing unction and sweating with due consideration of dosage and time. **[15]**

> मात्राकालाश्चया सुक्तः: । सिद्धियुक्ती प्रतिप्तिता । तिष्ठत्युष्चैर युक्तिज्ञो द्रव्यज्ञानवात् सदा ॥१६॥

Proper administration (of drugs) depends on dosage and time and success is based on proper administration. Hence, knower of proper administration always stand at the top among the knowers of drugs. **[16]**

> अत ऊर्ध्वं प्रवक्ष्यामि यवाग्वर्विविधौषधाः । विविधानां विकाराणां सत्तमानां निवृत्तये ॥१७॥

Further, (I) will speak about gruels (prepared with) various drugs for alleviations of various disorders amenable to them. **[17]**

> पिप्पलीपिप्पलीमूलचव्यचित्रकनागरै: । यवागूर्दीपनी साद्वाग्न्युल्लनी चोससाधिता ॥१८॥

The gruel prepared with pippalī, pippalīmūla, cavya, citraka and śuṇṭhī stimulates appetite and relieves (colic) pain. **[18]**

> दधिमस्तकपत्थाब्जैः रीतकादाविभिःसाधिता । पाचनी ग्राहिणी, पेया सप्तो पाङ्गमूलिक: ॥१९॥

The gruel prepared with kappittha, bilwa, cāṅgerī, buttermilk and pomegranates is digestive and astringent. The liquid gruel of smaller five roots[^1] is useful for diarrhoea with vāta. **[19]**

> ज्वालपत्नीतचीचित्ते: पृक्षिषय च साधिता । दाधिनाम्ता हिता पेया पित्तलेभ्वातिसारिणाम् ॥२०॥

The liquid gruel prepared with śālaparṇī, balā, bilwa, pṛśniparṇī and soured pomegranates is useful for diarrhoea and *śleṣmika* diarrhoea. **[20]**

> पयस्यधयेकः स्त्री ह्रीबेरोत्पलनामिः । पेया रक्तातिसारेटी पृच्छिपर्ण्य च साधिता ॥२१॥

The liquid gruel Processed with hrībera, utpala, musta and pṛśniparṇī and prepared with goat's milk having half water alleviates blood diarrhoea and is that prepared with pṛśniparṇī. **[21]**

> द्धाराक सतिलपिचैः पेयां साम तामत्त सनाराम् । इन्दुष्यठटलःचश्वर्गजीलयर्षा मूष्पलो समुलित्सानाम् ॥२२॥

One should prescribe the gruel prepared with atīviṣā and śuṇṭhī and soured (with pomegranates) in diarrhoea having āma. That of gokṣura and kaṇṭakārī alongwith phāṇita should be prescribed in dysuria. **[22]**

> विडङ्गपिप्पलीमूलशिग्रुभर्विरिवे च । तक्रसिद्धा वधाद्ज: स्यात् क्रिमिघ्न्ां ससुवर्चिका ॥२३॥

The gruel of viḍaṅga, pippalīmūla, śigru and marica prepared with buttermilk and having suvarcikā (a kind of alkali) alleviates *kṛmis* (helminths). **[23]**

> मृद्वीकासारिवालाजापिप्पलीमधुराहिगारैः । पिप्पलाणाम् विषघ्नो वा सौमराजीविष्पिचिता ॥२४॥

That prepared with drākṣā, sārivā, parched paddy, pippalī, honey and śuṇṭhī alleviates (excessive) thirst. The one prepared with somarājī is anti-poison. **[24]**

> दध्ना वराहनिर्वूहें यवाग्घूर्हणी मता । गवेधुकानां कृशानां कार्णीयो समाहिता ॥२५॥

The gruel prepared with pork juice is bulk-promoting. The one prepared with fried gavedhuka alongwith honey is emaciating. **[25]**

> सर्पिमता वहुतिला स्नेहनी लवणान्विता । श्यामाकमरकाण्ण्िभरुश्यानामुनतू चिरक्ष्णों ॥२६॥

The gruel prepared with profuse sesamum and added with ghee and salt provides unctuousness. The one of śyāmāka prepared with decoction of kuśa and āmalaka produces roughness. **[26]**

> दशमूलीकृतः कासहिक्काश्वासकफापहा । यमकेन मदिरासिद्धा पाकाशयसकापहा ॥२७॥

The one prepared with ten roots[^2] alleviates cough, hiccup, dyspnoea and kapha. The one prepared with dual fat (ghee and oil) alongwith madirā (wine) alleviates pain in colon. **[27]**

> शाकैर्मिसतिलमांसैः: सिद्धा षर्जों निरस्यति । जम्बवाम्रास्विपिधचाम्लसिद्धा । साङ्ग्राहिकी मता ॥२८॥

That prepared with potherbs, meat, sesamum and black gram evacuates bowels. That (prepared) with seeds of jambū and āmra (mango), sour kapittha and bilwa is astringent. **[28]**

> क्षारियवकहि्ङ्गयमनयवर्षेजेदेीनि मता । अम्रभापिप्पलीमूलार्विधिकर्वासातुलोमिनी ॥२९॥

That (prepared) with *yavakṣāra* (alkali obtained from barley plant), citraka, hiṅgu and amlavetasa is mass-breaking. The one with harītakī, pippalīmūla and śuṇṭhī helps carmination (passing of flatus). **[29]**

> तक्रसिद्धा यवागूः: स्याद् धृतव्यापत्तिनाशिनी । तैलव्यापदि शस्ता स्यात्त्वाम्लकापिष्ठकसाधिता ॥३०॥

The gruel prepared with buttermilk relieves of untoward effects caused by (excessive intake of) ghee. The one prepared with buttermilk and sesame paste is useful in conditions caused by (excessive intake of) oil. **[30]**

> गव्यमांसरसैः: सामा वियवाग्बानामिनी । कफाजपा यवानी यमके पिप्पलमालकैः ॥३१॥

The gruel prepared with beef juice and soured with pomegranates alleviates intermittent fevers. The one prepared from barley in ghee and oil with pippalī and āmalaka is useful for throat. **[31]**

> ताम्रचूडं से सिद्धा रेतोमार्गरजायदा । समाविदेदना शच्या वृहदीरोपसादिता ॥३२॥

That prepared in chicken juice alleviates pain in seminal passage. That with split black grams prepared in ghee and milk is aphrodisiac. **[32]**

> उपोदिकादिभिश्च सिद्धा मद्यविनाशिनी । क्षुपं हन्त्वासवायोगी हिरण्यमसारैः ॥३३॥

The one with upodikā and curd alleviates narcosis. That of apāmārga cooked with milk and juice of iguana meat destroys hunger. **[33]**

## Summing-up verses

> तत श्लोकाः–
> अष्टाविंशतिरित्येता यथागु: परिकीर्तिताः । पञ्च कर्माणि चान्क्रिम प्रोक्तो भैषज्यसंग्रहः ॥३४॥

Thus these twenty eight gruels have been described and the groups of drugs in connection with five measures have been said. **[34]**

> पूर्वं मूलकलेशानानहेलोष्क यष्टीचसम् । पङ्कक्कर्मावयज्ञानवेयस्तिल्कर्त् कीर्तितं पुनः ॥३५॥

The drugs, which were mentioned earlier for the sake of knowledge of their (useful) roots and fruits, have again been described (here) to indicate their participation in the five measures. **[35]**

> स्मृतिमान् हेतुयुक्तिज्ञो जितात्मा प्रतिपत्तिमान् । भिषग्योषसंयोगोत्तरिकित्सां कर्तुमर्हति ॥३६॥

The physician endowed with memory, proficient in rational management, having self-control and presence of mind is capable of treating (a patient) with combination of drugs. **[36]**

## Colophon

> इत्यग्निवेशकृते तन्त्रे चरकप्रतिसंस्कृते श्लोकस्थानेऽपामार्गतण्डुलीयो नाम द्वितीयोऽध्यायः ॥२॥

Thus ends the second chapter on seeds of apāmārga etc. in Ślokasthāna in the treatise composed by Agniveśa and redacted by Caraka. **(2)**

[^1]: *Śālaparṇī, pṛśniparṇī, bṛhatī, kaṇṭakārī* and *gokṣura*.
[^2]: *Bilwa, agnimantha, śyonāka, gambhārī, pāṭalā, śālaparṇī, pṛśniparṇī, bṛhatī, kaṇṭakārī* and *gokṣura*. The first five are called as bigger roots (*bṛhat pañcamūla*) and the latter ones as smaller roots (*laghu pañcamūla*). Both together make *daśamūla* (ten roots).
