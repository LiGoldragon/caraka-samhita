27. In the context of discussing similarity between Loka (other word) and Puruṣa (person) Caraka says that as Loka is constituted by six constituents such as five mahābhūtas and the subtle Brahman so is the Person composed of these six factors¹. Here the "Puruṣa" has been repeated while the first one should have been replaced by the word "Loka". It is surprising that the teachers have been teaching it as it is sometimes even defending its propriety.

28. In causes of abortion, looking to the context the word "विच्यवान" should be replaced by "विच्यवान" which is followed by the words "स्थान" and "डान" (C.Śā.8.24).

29. The use of the words "क्षीपुसंयोग्यापाससशोलितगर्भशरुयायः" (C.Śā. 8. 3) is not correct. It should be read as "क्षीपुसंयोग्यापाससशोलितगर्भशरुयायः" (also in C.Śā. 3. 4). Caraka is very cautious in this respect² and it is improbable that the above usage represents him genuinely. Hence it may be taken as textual error and as such should be rectified.

30. In the reksoghna group of drugs the word "श्लेष्मक" should be read as "हेमक" meaning coraka because ataśī is already mentioned there separately (S.Śā. 8. 47).

31. In "उपानत्पुनात्तलस्य प्रयत्नः पादवर्णौः:" (C. In. 5. 34) "पादवर्णौः:" should be read as "पादवर्णौः:" meaning protective cover of the feet like socks. Cakrapāṇi has not commented on this. The recent translators have rendered it as 'peeling of skin of the feet' which does not seem as appropriate. Similarly, "वर्म विध्युतृ" (C. In. 12. 19) should be read as "वर्म विध्युतृ".

32. "आआईस्त" (C. In. 11. 21) should be read as "आआम्ता".
