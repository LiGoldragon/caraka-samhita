[Devanagari unclear: verse on intellect]

Derangement of intellect, restraint, and memory; advent of time and action
and contact with unsuitable sense objects should be known as the causes of
misery. [98]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on absence of will]

Adherence to this not without discrimination between eternal and non-eternal as
well as wholesome and unwholesome is known as derangement of intellect because
the intellect sees rightly. [99]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on viparyastasmṛti]

Due to derangement of restraint it is not possible to control the mind indulged in
sensual pleasures from the unwholesome object because the fuction of restraint is to
control. [100]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on smṛtivibhraṃśa]

When on covering of the self by rajas and tamas the recollection of the knowl-
edge of reality is impaired, it is known as derangement of memory because the thing
to be recollected is deposited in memory. [101]
