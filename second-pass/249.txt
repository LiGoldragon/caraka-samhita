Madirā (clear wine), jagala (yeast wine) and rough surā produce intoxication
quickly and produces moistening, watering, softening and burning in mouth. That
which, on contact, irritates and produces piercing pain in tongue and stimulates secre-
tions with burning from mouth, nose and eyes is pungent. That which, on contact with
tongue, destroys all other gustatory perceptions so that no other taste is perceived;
and also gives rise to non-sliminess and dryness in mouth, alongwith cheerfulness, is
tikta (rasa). Kaṣāya is that which produces non-sliminess, stiffness and coldness in
tongue, as if choking the throat and is also depressent. [73-79]

[Devanagari unclear: more verses on alcoholic drinks]

Various types of wine, sūrāmaṇḍa (clear surā wine), prasanna (clear surā), kapiśa
(reddish surā), jagala, medaka, sīdhu (a fermented preparation of sugarcane juice),
āsava (fermented liquid prepared from sugarcane juice etc.), ariṣṭa (a fermented liq-
uid prepared from medicated decoction), māhva (mahuā wine), madhwāsava (honey
wine), maireya (a fermented liquor of sugar etc.), various flower wines, fruit wines,
varuṇī wine (palm wine)–these are alcoholic drinks.

Pure water of well, river, pond, rain water, melted from snow or hail, water of
sweet root–all these are useful for normal patients, having no aggravation of doṣa, alle-
viate fatigue, satisfy thirst, are cordial, easy in digestion. Water with salt, hot, is appe-
tising, lights ama, laghu, increases agni and reduces kapha. Cold water alleviates
fainting, fatigue, exhaustion, heat, alcoholic complications, vomiting, internal haem-
orrhage, biliousness, bleeding, narcosis and pittaja disorders.

The water cooled with sandalwood pieces, śīta, sugandhi etc., the water cooled
in earthen pot, water from haṃsodaka (water boiled and cooled with star agastya),
hot water boiled and reduced to one-fourth–these are recommended for use as drink
for various conditions.
