When, in the event of diminution of kapha, vāta carries away the normal pitta
from its location, it produces unstable tearing pain, burning sensation in those organs
wherever it spreads alongwith fatigue and debility.

When in the state of diminution of pitta, the dominant vāta drags on kapha, it
causes pain, cold sensation, stiffness and heaviness. When pitta, in diminution of kapha,
obstructs the normal vāta burning sensation arises.

When pitta, in diminution of vāta, blocks the normal kapha, it produces drowsi-
ness, heaviness and fever.

When aggravated kapha, in diminution of pitta, creates obstruction to vāta, it
produces shivering, heaviness and fever.
