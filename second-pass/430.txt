[Devanagari unclear: verse on six groups]

Thus six groups of (drugs used in) non-unctuous enema have been described
according to rasas for characterization according to name on the basis of individual
rasas. According to predominance and otherwise of rasas, conjunction and disjunc-
tion combination of rasas should be ascertained. The proportional variations in com-
bined rasas can not be enumerated. Hence the wise should know about (the prepara-
tion of) non-unctuous enema in respect of nature, agent, instrument, action,
result, after-effect, place, time, procedure, indication and contra-indication; and
should manage these factors with rationale. Whatever is contradicted in this con-
text, that should be discarded; whatever is not so, but is admissible, should be
adopted; whatever has not been said in this respect should be inferred on the
basis of similar (treatment) without contradiction. Thus the basic factors leading
to the success of an action should be considered first and after their thorough
examination one should proceed for the action. [145]
