[Devanagari unclear: verse on Upālambha]

Upālambha–Upālambha is pointing out defects in causality as explained earlier
under the heading "hetu" fallacious or any other reason. [59]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Parihāra]

Parihāra–Parihāra (refutation) is refuting the above pointing out of defects.
Such as the signs of life are available constantly till the self is in the body, and are
not available after it goes out. Therefore, the self is different from the body and
is eternal. [60]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Pratijñāhāni]

Pratijñāhāni–Pratijñāhāni (loss of proposition) is that when one gives up the origi-
nal proposition after having been questioned (by the opponent). For instance, some-
body puts proposition as 'the personal self is eternal' in the beginning but when ques-
tioned says that as non-eternal. [61]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Abhyanujñā]

Abhyanujñā–Abhyanujñā (admission of argument) is acceptance of the desired
as well as the undesired situations. [62]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Hetwantara]

Hetwantara–Hetwantara (imperfect reason) is that where some other thing is
stated instead of perfect reason. [63]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Arthāntara]

Arthāntara–Arthāntara (irrelevant statement) is that where some other thing is
said instead of the relevant one. For instance, one has to say the symptoms of fever
but instead he says those of prameha. [64]
