218                           CARAKA-SAṂHITĀ                             [CH

The edibles prepared with jaggery, sesamum, milk, honey and sugar are aphro-
disiac, strength-promoting and exceedingly heavy. [268-270]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on dietary preparations of wheat]

Various types of edibles prepared of wheat with addition of fat or cooked in fat
are heavy, saturating, aphrodisiac and cordial.

Edibles prepared of wheat and its flour such as fried grains, parpaṭa, pūpa
etc. are light because of processing. Their properties may be indicated accor-
dingly. [271-272]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on pṛthuka, yāva]

Pṛthuka (flattened rice) is heavy and health-promoting, is not light, has plenty of
a little quantity.

Yāva (a preparation of barley) digests with distension but if taken with some
soup it is purgative. [273]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on legume preparations]

The preparations of legumes aggravate vāta as they are rough and cold. They
should be taken in small quantity after adding with pungent spices, fat and salt. [274]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on edibles with mild cooking]

The edibles which are bulky and hard are heavy if mildly cooked and nourishing
and strength-promoting if overcooked. [275]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on heaviness or lightness of edibles]

Heaviness or lightness of edibles should be decided after knowing the main in-
gredient, combination of other ingredients, processing and quantity of different in-
gredients. [276]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on rasālā preparation]

(Vimardaka prepared of various types of substances–ripe, unripe, moistened and
fried is heavy, cordial, aphrodisiac and suitable for the strong. (277)

रसाला बृहणी वृष्या निव्र्या बल्या रुचिर्या । स्नेहन हृयं कुम्भं वातानां सपूदं दषि ।।२७८।।

Rasālā is bulk-promoting, aphrodisiac, unctuous, cordial, and alleviates vāta. [278]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on curd added with jaggery]

Curd added with jaggery is unctuous, saturating, cordial, and alleviates vāta. [278]
