द्वितीयोऽध्यायः

CHAPTER II

अथातस्त्रिविमकुक्षीयं विमानं व्याख्यास्यामः ॥१॥

Now (I) shall expound the chapter on the specific features of three portioned
belly. [1]

इति ह स्माह भगवानात्रेयः ॥२॥

As propounded by Lord Ātreya. [2]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on three portions of belly]

The consumer of food should make room for the food in the belly in three por-
tion–such as one portion of the space for solid food items, one for liquids and one for
vāta, pitta and kapha. While taking food in such quantity, one is not inflicted with any
harmful effect caused by food taken in inappropriate quantity. [3]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on inappropriate quantity]

It is not possible to derive entire beneficial effects of food on the basis of its
quantity because all the eight factors of the method of dieting such as nature etc. have
their divided effects. [4]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on quantity of food]
