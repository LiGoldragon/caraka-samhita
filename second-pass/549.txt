The person with his eyes slanted upwards, and carotid regions constantly trem-
bling alongwith debility, thirst and dryness of mouth does not survive.

If a patient has prominent cheeks (zygomatics), severe fever and cough, colic
and dislike for food is not amenable to treatment.

If a patient has head, tongue and mouth revolved, eye brows dropped and tongue
thorny he is just like a ghost.

If the penis is excessively shrunken and testicles too much pendulous or vice
versa, it indicates patient's death.

The patient whose muscles are wasted and is thus reduced to skin and skeleton,
who is too weak and does not take food, lives utmost for a month. [27-31]
