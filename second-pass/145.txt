[Devanagari unclear: Lord Ātreya's reply on counter-action]

Lord Ātreya replied–Agniveśa! It is possible for me or person like me to coun-
teract in such a way that the treatment succeeds invariably and also to impart instruction
in proper methods of administration, but there is none here to compete for receiving
these instructions or thereafter deciding a course of action or administering the rem-
edy; because the variations in morbidity, drug, place, time, strength, body,
diet, suitability, mind, constitution and age are quite subtle which, while being con-
sidered, confuse the mind of even great scholars who in turn say of those having low
intelligence. Hence, we shall discuss both well-proper administration of therapy and
in case of failure, management of complications later on in siddhisthāna. [6]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on equipment arrangement]

Now (I) shall tell briefly about the equipments, though of various types, such as–
the one expert in architecture should, first of all, arrange for an auspicious house
which should be strong, wind-free, ventilating, having comfortable moving space, not
situated in valley, inaccessible to smoke, the sun, water, dust, sound, sight and smell
and provided with water-reservoir, mortar-pestle, lavatory, bathroom and kitchen. [7]
