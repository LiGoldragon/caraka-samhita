XXX]                          SŪTRASTHĀNA                                  237

[Devanagari unclear: verses on the heart]

In the heart attached are ten vessels rooted there and of great significance. The
words 'mahat', 'artha' and 'hṛdaya' are synonymous. The body with six divisions, intel-
lect, sense organs, five sense objects, self together with qualities, alongwith its
objects are located in heart. [3-4]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on heart specialists]

The heart specialists regard the heart as the substratum of these entities like the
central girder of the small beams in a house. One gets mūrcchana (fainting) on its
injury and death on its severe painful condition. [5]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on heart and consciousness]

If that suspended in air, the activity of the senses are exhibited in this house if it is
fully active, then comes the field of consciousness. That is why the heart has been
said as 'mahat' (great) and 'artha' (serving all purposes) by the physicians. [6-7]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on great vessels in heart]

From the heart as root, ten great vessels carrying ojas pulsate all over the
body. [8]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on ojas]

The ojas, which maintains the living beings by its saturation; without which no
life of creatures exists, which is the initial essence of embryo and also the essence
of its nourishing material, which enters into the cardiac cycle first, which destroyed leads
to destruction, which is the sustainer and located in heart, which is the cream of the
nutrient fluid in the body, and where vital factors are established, is the fruit of them
or they produce various types of fruits (effects). Hence they (vessels) are called as
'mahāphalā' (having important fruit or great variety of fruits). [9-11]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on dhamani]

Dhamanis (arteries) are (called so) due to pulsation, srotas (channels) due to
flowing and sirās (veins) due to moving slowly. [12]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on great vessels]

The ten great vessels are not different from these. They have only difference in
nomenclature, location and function. The ten heart-rooted vessels nourish the body. [13]
