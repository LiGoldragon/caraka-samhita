[Devanagari unclear: long verse on aggregate]

Ākāśa etc. (ākāśa, vāyu, tejas, ap and pṛthivī), buddhi (mahat–intellect), avyakta
(the unmanifest primordial nature) and ahaṅkāra (ego), the eighth one, constitute
bhūtaprakṛti (original source of creatures). Vikāras (products) are sixteen–five sense
organs, five motor organs, mind and five objects (bhūtas). [63-64]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on this aggregate as unmanifest]

This (aggregate) except the unmanifest one is known as kṣetra (body). The
unmanifest one is known as the knower of this body by the sages. [65]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on intellect evolves]

From the unmanifest (primordial nature) intellect evolves, then ego comes forth,
from ego five bhūtas emanate in order. Thus evolved and complete with all the organs
the person is born and is said as emerged. [66]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on dissolution]

At the time of dissolution, the self gets detached from these enjoyable entities.
Thus (the self), subdued by rajas and tamas, gets manifested from the unmanifest
state and then reverts to the unmanifest and thus moves continuously like a wheel
(from one state to another). Those who are attached excessively to duals and are
engrossed by ego are subjected to origin and destruction but not others who are
contrary to them. [67-69]
