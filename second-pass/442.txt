[Devanagari unclear: long verse on signs]

Mind is unconscious but active while the self without kindless consciousness (but is inac-
tive). The omnipresent one becomes active only when is comes in contact with mind.
The self is said as 'doer' because of having consciousness while mind, devoid of con-
sciousness, is not taken as such inspite of his activeness. [75-76]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on prāṇa]

All by his respective self leads himself to all the species attaining his goal and
controlling with Prāṇa, none else is his master. [77]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on free to fruit]

The self is all powerful (free) to initiate some action but is obliged to enjoy its
fruit. The powered one concentrate his mind and also can renunciate all. [78]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on going to body]

The self, though omnipresent, when puts on a body becomes restricted to his
own tactile sense organ. That is why, he is incapable of perceiving all senses and
those pertaining to all bodies. [79]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on omnipresent]

The self is omnipresent because he is all-pervasive and great. Hence if he con-
centrates his mind he can perceive even the hidden (object). That associated for ever
with mind in accordance with the action of the body should be taken as pervading all
species even though (apparently) stationed in some species. [80-81]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on initial origin]

There is no beginning of the self and the continuity of the (created) body. Hence,
both being beginningless, it is impossible to say which is earlier. [82]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on knowing self]

Only the one having knowledge can be witness not the ignorant one that is why
the self is said as an observer. All the entities of all the living beings are observed by
the self. [83]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on no abnormality]

These are the causes of painful sensations. The cause of happiness (health) is
only one–balanced use (of the senses) which is very rare. [84]
