[Devanagari unclear: verses on kulmāṣa]

Kulmāṣa (boiled and spiced grains) is heavy, rough, vāta-aggravating and purga-
tive. The physician should determine heaviness and otherwise of other boiled grains
such as legumes, wheat and barley. [260-261]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on yūṣa (soup)]

In unspiced and spiced vegetable soups, mildly spiced and heavily spiced
meat soup, and sound and un-soured pulses, the heaviness increases in progressive
order. [262]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on śālī rice products]

Flour of roasted grains aggravates vāta, is rough, produces abundant faeces and
is carminative. Taken in liquid form, it saturates the person immediately and provides
strength quickly. That prepared of śālī rice is sweet, light, cold, constipating and alle-
viates internal haemorrhage, thirst, vomiting and fever. [263-264]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on apūpa, yāvaka, vāṭya]

Apūpa, yāvaka and vāṭya made of barley remove the diseases such as udāvarta,
coryza, cough, prameha and throat disorders.

Germinated grains, fried grains, śaṣkulī, madhukroda, piṇḍaka, pūpa, pūpalikā
etc. are heavy; the preparations of (rice) flour are heaviest. [265-267]
