XXVII]                        SŪTRASTHĀNA                                  195

[Devanagari unclear: verses on śyāmāka, koradūṣa]

Śyāmāka alongwith koradūṣa is astringent-sweet, light, aggravates vāta and allevi-
ates kapha and pitta, is cold, constipating and absorbent.

Hastiṣyāmāka, niwara, toyaparṇī, gavedhuka, praśantikā, ambahaṣyāmāka,
lauhitya, anu, priyaṅgu, mukunda, jhiṇṭī, garmūṭi, varuka, varaka, śimbira, utkaṭa,
jūrṇa–these are similar to śyāmāka in properties. [16-18²]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on yava (barley)]

Yava (barley) is rough, cold, non-heavy, sweet, producing abundance of flatus
and faeces, promotes stability, is slightly astringent, tonic and alleviates disorders of
kapha. Veṇuyaya (barley-shaped seeds of bamboo) is rough, having astringent as su2-
sidiary rasa, sweet, alleviates kapha and pitta and also fat, helminths and poisons, and
is strength-promoting. [19-20]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on godhūma (wheat)]

Godhūma (wheat) is union-promoting, vāta-alleviating, sweet, cold, vitaliser,
bulk-promoting, aphrodisiac, unctuous, stabiliser and heavy. Nāndīmukhī and madhūlī
(types of wheat) are sweet, unctuous and cold.

Thus ends the first group of awned cereals. [21-22]
