III]                          ŚĀRĪRASTHĀNA                                   421

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Ātreya's reply]

Lord Ātreya said–'no', set these entities combined together give rise to
embryo. (5)

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on mother's contribution]

The embryo is produced by mother. There can't be origin of embryo without mother
nor can there be birth of viviparous beings. The entities which are particularly
maternal (skin, blood, flesh, fat, navel, heart, kloma, liver, spleen, kidneys, urinary
bladder, colon, stomach, intestine, rectum, anus, small intestines, large intestines,
omentum and mesentery) are derived from mother. [6]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on father's contribution]

The embryo is produced from father, there can not be origin of embryo nor
can there be birth of viviparous beings. The entities which are particularly pater-
nal and are derived from father are these such as–head hairs, beards-mustaches,
nails, body hairs, teeth, bones, veins, ligaments and semen are derived from
father). [7]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on self contribution]
