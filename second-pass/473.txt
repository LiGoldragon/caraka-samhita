[Devanagari unclear: verse on description of morbidity]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on absolute self]

The absolute self is devoid of abnormality and characters in all beings, hence the
characters are found only due to specific features of psyche and body. [33]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on dośas]

There are three doṣas in body–vāta, pitta and kapha. They affect the body. Two
are the doṣas in psyche–rajas and tamas. They affect the psyche when psyche or body
or both is/are affected, the morbidity arises otherwise not. [34]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on body psyche four types]

The body has already been said as of four types according to species. [35]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on psyche]

The psyche is said as of three types–pure, rājas and tāmasa. The pure is said as devoid of
defects due to having beneficial fraction whereas rājasa and tāmasa are defective be-
cause of the fractions of agitation and ignorance respectively. Out of these three types
of psyche, each one has got innumerable subdivisions due to relative degree and vari-
ation in interaction of psyche and body according to species. Body follows psyche
and vice versa. Hence some of the types of psyche are described here as illustrations
by showing their similarity with some well-known symbols. (36)
