XXVIII]                       SŪTRASTHĀNA                                  229

[Devanagari unclear: verse on māṃsa-doṣa]

Now, listen about diseases caused by affection of māṃsa. Adhimāṃsa, arbuda (tu-
mour), kīla, galaśālūka, galaśuṇḍikā, pūtimāṃsa, alajī, gaṇḍa, cervical adenitis,
upajihvikā–these are located in māṃsa. [13-14]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on medas-doṣa]

Now, listen about those caused by affection of māṃsa. The despicable
ones and the premonitory symptoms of prameha. These are the despicable
ones and the premonitory symptoms of prameha. [15]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on asthi-doṣa]

Further, I will say about those located in medas. These are the despicable
ones and the premonitory symptoms of prameha. [15]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on majjā-doṣa]

Adhyasthi, adhidanta, dantabheda, asthiśūla, discoloration, abnormality in hairs
of head, body hairs, nails, beards-moustaches–these are disorders due to morbid
affection of asthi. [16]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on majjā-doṣa]

Pain in joints, giddiness, fainting, feeling of darkness and appearance of thick-
based wounds–these are caused due to morbid affection of majjā. [17]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on śukra-doṣa and progeny]

By the morbid affection of śukra, there will be impotency and non-erection of
penis. His progeny will be sick or impotent or short-lived or deformed. There is more
likelihood that the conception will not take place or if conceived, it will be miscarried
or aborted. The semen, if affected morbidly, afflicts the person alongwith his wife
and progeny. [18-19]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on sense organs]

When doṣas vitiate in the locations of sense organs, they afflict them with loss of
function and dysfunction. [20]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on snāyu-srotas-doṣa]

Doṣas vitiated in ligaments, vessels and tendons afflict the person with stiffness,
contraction, weakness, glands, throbbing and numbness. [21]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on mala-doṣa]

Doṣas vitiated in malas, cause breaking, drying up and other abnor-
malities of malas (excretions) and also their excessive retention and elimination. [22]
