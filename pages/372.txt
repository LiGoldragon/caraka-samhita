Swedavaha srotas are affected due to (excessive) physical exercise, exces-
sive heat, use of hot and cold things not in order, and also due to anger, grief and
fear. [10-22]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on diet and behaviour effects]

Food and behaviour which are similar to doṣas and dissimilar to dhātus in pro-
perties cause morbidity in srotas. [23]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on enhanced flow]

Enhanced flow or obstruction or formation of nodules and flow of contents in
wrong direction–these are symptoms of morbidity of srotas. [24]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on srotas characteristics]

Srotas have colour similar to that of their own dhātus, are tubular, large or small;
long and branch like in shape. [25]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on treatment]

Treatment of affected Prāṇavaha, udakavaha and annavaha srotas is that prescribed
for bronchial asthma, polydypsia and āmapradoṣa. In morbidity of the srotas for rasa
etc., the treatment mentioned in the chapter on various food and drinks should be ap-
plied in respective disorders. In disorder of mūtravaha, purīṣavaha and swedavaha
srotas the treatment adopted should be the same as in dysuria, diarrohoea and fever
respectively. [26-28]

Now (the summing up) verses–

[Devanagari unclear: summary verse]

Roots of thirteen srotas, general symptoms of their disorder, synonyms, morbid-
ity of srotas and dhātus, etiological factors separately and also the treatment, basic
facts about srotas–all this has been described in this chapter on srotas. [29-31]

One to whom is known the body from all aspects and also all the bodily diseases
does not get confused in action. [29-31]
