[Devanagari unclear: long verse on prodroma]

The three doṣas vitiated and about to produce pramehas exhibit these prodromal
symptoms–such as, matting of hairs, sweetness in mouth, numbness and burning sen-
sation in hands and feet, dryness in mouth, palate and throat, thirst, lassitude, dirt in
the body, smearing in body orifices, burning sensation and numbness in body parts,
crawling of bees and ants on the body and urine, morbidities in urine, fleshy smell in
body, frequent sleep and drowsiness. [47]

भवति चात्र–

Here are (the verses)–

[Devanagari unclear: verse on greedy patients]

Prameha approaches immediately like a bird to its nest-tree the person who is
greedy in eatables and has dislike for bath and walking.

Death, in the form of prameha, takes away immediately the person who is dull in
activities, over-obese, over-uncted and voracious eater.

The person who takes food which maintains the equilibrium of dhātus and also
practises various physical activities enjoys happy life. [50-52]
