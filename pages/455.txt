[Devanagari unclear: verse on whence disorders]

Whenceforth the disease? what is their remedy? What is the cause of happi-
ness and sorrow? How should the physical and mental disorders once subdued not
recur? [39]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on three causes]

Intellectual error, unbalanced use of senses and consequence of time these three
are the causes of all the disorders. Similarly, the remedy of all disorders consists of
the balanced use of intellect, senses and time. [40]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on virtues]

Virtuous acts bring forth happiness while the contrary ones lead to sorrow. Source
of diseases is body and mind, when these (body and mind) are absent, the diseases can
not recur. [41]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on continuity]

The continuity of physique and psyche is beginningless. Their absence can be
brought out only by Excellent treatment, memory and knowledge. [42]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on twofold seats]

Even though the above-mentioned twofold seats of diseases exist, one should
always take precautions against diseases. One having overcome the senses can not
fall a prey to diseases except predestined at the moment. [43]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on daiva and pauruṣa]

The deeds of the previous life are known as daiva (fate) and of the present
life as pauruṣa (personal effort). These in unbalanced way cause diseases while in
balanced one avert them. [44]
