[Devanagari unclear: long verse on yogic powers]

Entering into other's body, knowledge of other's mind, doing things at pleasure,
supernatural vision, supernatural hearing, superhuman memory, superhuman brilliance
and disappearance at pleasure–these eight are said as supper-natural powers of yogins.
All this is possible only due to concentration of pure mind in the self. [140-141]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on emancipation]

Mokṣa (emancipation) is possible by absence of rajas and tamas, destruction of
the potent past deeds and detachment from all (the sources of) conjunction. It is also
said as absence of rebirth. [142]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on emancipation factors]

Entering into the saints, avoidance of the ignobles, observance of vows,
fasting and other rules, study of religious scriptures, understanding, attachment to
lonely places and detachment from worldly pleasure, effort for emancipation, great
restraint non-initiation of action, destruction of past deeds, inclination to getting out,
freedom from ego, realizing fear in conjunction, concentration of mind and body,
critical analysis of the philosophical reality–all this arises due to emergence of the
memory of reality (which leads to emancipation). [143-146]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on memory]

Memory comes forth by the factors beginning with keeping in touch with the
saints and ending with restraint. After emergence of memory one gets rid of misery
by recollecting the nature of created beings. [147]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on emergence of memory]

(Now I) will mention the eight factors which help in emergence of memory. These
are–perception of the cause, perception of the form, similarity, contrast, predomi-
nance of sattva, practice, constant thinking and repeated hearing. Memory is defined
as recollection of seen, heard and experienced. [148-149]
