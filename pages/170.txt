[Devanagari unclear: continued verses on swelling locations and types]

The swelling located in the abdomen with foul smelling discharge of urine and
faeces is rohiṇī (a type of swelling).

When vitiated doṣas remain located in the chest, it produces vidradhi (an internal
abscess).

The swelling caused by combination of all the three doṣas in joints and bones is
called sandhigatāḍhya.

If the swelling is located in the entire body, it is generalised, otherwise it is
localised.

By such variations, the types of swellings are classified.

Treatment of swellings is described in the chapters on respective conditions–
gulma, vidradhi, oedema, prameha, leprosy etc. The general treatment is also de-
scribed here.

The treatment of swelling consists of fasting, lightening therapy (vomiting,
purgation), bloodletting, application of pastes, bandaging, fomentation, evacuation
of stagnant doṣas, restoration of digestive power and so on. The diet should be light
and digestive.

[Devanagari unclear: closing verses on chapter summary]

तत्र श्लोकाः–

Now (the summing up) verses–

The two types of swelling–innate and exogenous, three types caused by doṣas,
seven types based on causation, etiology and symptoms of the seven types, prognosis,
complications and treatment principle have been described in the chapter on three
swellings.

इत्यग्निवेशकृते तन्त्रे चरकप्रतिसंस्कृते श्लोकस्थाने त्रिशोथीयो
नाम अष्टादशोऽध्यायः ॥१८॥

Thus ends the eighteenth chapter on three swellings in Ślokasthāna in the
treatise composed by Agniveśa and redacted by Caraka. (18)
