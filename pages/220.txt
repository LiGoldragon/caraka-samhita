Excellence and non-excellence are determined according to place, time, age, mode
of cooking, potency, taste, suitability, doṣa and drug.

Rationality is the application of intellect to determine the dose, time, action and
combination of substances and the conducting of treatment.

Enumeration is the counting up of various entities.

Conjunction is the combining together of one or more substances permanently or
in moderation.

Disjunction is the dividing of substances and their separation.

Separateness is the difference of one substance from another.

Measure means the standard quantification of substance.

Transformation refers to qualitative changes brought about in substances through
processes of cooking etc.

Practice is the constant or repeated use of a substance.

Thus the ten properties known as parādi are explained which serve as well-known
causal factors in therapeutics. [29-31]
