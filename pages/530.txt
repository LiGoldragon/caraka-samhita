[Devanagari unclear: long verse on factors of normal constitution]

Normal constitution is governed by several factors such as birth (genetic factor),
family, place, time, age and individuality. The entities of the persons are determined
by these factors. [5]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on morbidity]

Morbidity is of three types–that caused by bodily marks, that caused by diseases
and that caused by other factors. [6]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on bodily marks]

The first type of morbidity is that which is caused by the bodily marks due to
past deeds. Certain bodily marks are fixed in some body parts which produce abnor-
mality by getting opportunity in favourable times. (1)

[Devanagari unclear: verse on second type]

The second type of morbidity is produced by obvious diseases which are de-
scribed in the chapter on diagnosis in respect of their etiology. (2)

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on third type]

The third type of the abnormal complexions in nails, eyes, face, urine, fae-
ces, hands, feet, lips etc. particularly in patients with diminished strength, complex-
ion and sense indicates the loss of life. [13]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on similarity]

Similarly, on this line, malaise and cheerfulness, roughness and unctuousness

[Devanagari unclear: verse on aniṣṭa]

In the same way, appearance of any one of mole, freckles, black mole, and pim-
ple on the face of the patient should be taken as inauspicious. [12]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on appearance]

Appearance of any of the abnormal complexions in nails, eyes, face, urine, fae-
ces, hands, feet, lips etc. particularly in patients with diminished strength, complex-
ion and sense indicates the loss of life. [13]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on sudden complexions]

Such other abnormal complexions too which appear suddenly on the first
time without any apparent cause in the patient going down constantly are the signs of
death. [14]
