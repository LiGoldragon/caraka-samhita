I]                            ŚĀRĪRASTHĀNA                                  399

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on action of mind]

The sense-object is received by sense organs alongwith mind. Then the mind
analyses it in forms of merits or demerits and forwards it to Buddhi (intellect) which
produces decisive knowledge by which one proceeds to speak or to do something
with full knowledge. [22-23]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on five sense organs]

The five sense organs are composed of five mahābhūtas (Ākāśa etc.) with pre-
dominance of one in each. They are known by inference through their actions and it is
they on the basis of which intellect proceeds. [24]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on five motor organs]

Likewise, motor organs are also five in number such as hands, feet, anus, geni-
tals, and organ of speech. The first set are responsible for movement from one place to
another, anus and genitals are meant for discharging, hands for receiving and holding.
Tongue represents the organs of speech. The true speech is light while the false speech
is darkness. [25-26]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on five mahābhūtas]

The (five) mahābhūtas are ākāśa, vāyu, agni, ap and pṛthvī. Sound, touch, vi-
sion, taste and smell are their properties respectively. [27]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on properties of bhūtas]

Out of them, the first one has only one property and there is addition of one
more property in each of the latter bhūtas. Thus the former bhūta alongwith its property
is associated with the latter one. [28]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on properties of bhūtas]

Coarseness, liquidity, mobility, hotness and non-interruption are the specific char-
acters of pṛthvī, ap, vāyu, tejas and ākāśa respectively. All these characters are per-
ceivable by the tactile sense organ because touch alongwith its absence is known by
the tactile sense organ. The properties and the characters (in the body) of these
which possess properties (dravyas) are mentioned. [29-30]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on artha]

The sense objects, sound etc. are known as artha (sense objects). 'Gocara', 'viṣaya'
and 'guṇa' are synonyms. [31]
