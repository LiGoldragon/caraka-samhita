If severe diseases having conflicting sources of origin and treatment aggravate
with galloping speed, the patient will die soon.

If there is instantaneous reduction of strength, understanding, digestion, flesh
and blood, it indicates immediate death.

If one loses his health and normal constitution he is to die suddenly. [21-24]

[Devanagari unclear: summing verse]

Now the summing up verse–

These are the physical abnormalities which should be discarded. The wise do not
find any success with treatment in these conditions. [25]

इत्यग्निवेशकृते तन्त्रे चरकप्रतिसंस्कृते इन्द्रियस्थाने कतमानिशरीरीयमिन्द्रियं

Thus ends the sixth chapter on physical abnormalities in Indriyasthāna

सप्तमोऽध्यायः

CHAPTER VII

अथातः पन्नरूपीयमिन्द्रियं व्याख्यास्यामः ॥१॥

Now (I) shall expound the chapter on shadow and lustre. [1]

इति ह स्माह भगवानात्रेयः ॥२॥

As propounded by Lord Ātreya. [2]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on reflecting images]

If the patient, in both eyes, has pupil alongwith iris with no vision at all though
reflecting images, should not be treated. [3]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on deformity]

If there is any deformity observed in body parts in the shadow of the person
in moonlight, sunlight, light of a lamp, water or mirror, he should be taken as
ghost. [4]
