[Devanagari unclear: verse on Ahetu]

Ahetu-Ahetu (fallacious reason) is of three types–prakaraṇasama (similar to
topic), saṃśayasama, (similar to doubt) and varṇyasama (similar to object). The rea-
son similar to topic such as–the self other than the body is, eternal, as the body is non-
external the self should also be external. This fallacious reason because non-external
the pakṣa (minor term) itself has been used as reason. The fallacious reason similar to
doubt is that which though being cause of doubt is used as the cause of removing the
same. The reason similar to object is that which is not different from the object such
as somebody says–'Buddhi (intellect) is non-eternal because of the absence of touch
like śabda, (sound). Here both buddhi (intellect) and śabda are objects, hence because
of the absence of difference between them, the reason is similar to object and as such is
fallacious. [57]
