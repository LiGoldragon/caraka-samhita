[Devanagari unclear: verse on ṛṣyajihva]

Rough, reddish, blackish in interior and exterior, with blue, yellow and coppery
lustre, fast developing and decaying with little itching, oozing and organisms but in-
tense burning, tingling, piercing (with suppuration) having pain as if injured with bris-
tles, with elevated centre and their margins; pervaded with coarse boils, having large
circumference and shape like the tongue of the white-footed antelope is known as
Ṛṣyajihva. (4)

[Devanagari unclear: verse on puṇḍarīka]

White and red in lustre, with red margins pervaded with red lines and veins,
protuberant, with copious thick blood, pus and lymph, having fast development, organ-
isms, heat and suppuration, with fast development, causation and decay, similar to
petals of lotus is known as puṇḍarīka kuṣṭha. (5)

[Devanagari unclear: verse on sidhmakuṣṭha]

Rough and reddish, with thin and withered periphery, unctuous internally, with
white red lustre, numerous, having little pain, itching, heat, pus, lymph, having
slight causation and little decay and organisms, similar to bottle-gourd flowers is known
as sidhmakuṣṭha. (6)

[Devanagari unclear: verse on kākanaka]

Having colour like that of guñjā seeds initially but later on associated with symp-
toms of all sinful kuṣṭhas and consequently having multiple colours is known as kākaṇa.
This is incurable while others are curable. [8]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on incurability]

Here, it is incurable does not give us the incurability, however, some of
the curables, sometimes transgress their curability due to unwholesome regimen.
The six curable ones except kākanaka attain incurability if they are not treated or
unwholesome regimen is followed which leads them to the condition of abhiṣyanda
by doṣas. [9]
