Those predominant in properties of light, rough, coarse, non-slimy, minute
and touch are vāyavya (constituted predominantly by vāyu bhūta). They produce
actions of roughness, depression, movement, non-sliminess and lightness.

Dravyas having predominance in the properties of soft, light, minute, smooth
and sound are ākāśīya (constituted predominantly by ākāśa bhūta). They exert actions
of softening, no lumping and lightness. [11]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on no substance not used as drug]

On this basis there is no substance in the universe which can not be used as drug
on the condition that they are used rationally and with a definite objective. [12]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on drug actions]

The drugs are active not only due to properties but also due to their own intrinsic
composition, properties and both combined together in particular time, on reaching a
particular locus, with a particular mechanism and objective. The actions that perform is
karma (action), by means of which they act is vīrya (potency), where they act is
adhikaraṇa (locus of action), when they act is kāla (time), how they act is upāya (mecha-
nism) and what they achieve is result. [13]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on rasa variations]

There are sixty three types of variations of rasas according to substance, place
and time, that is mentioned (below). [14]
