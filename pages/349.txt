[Devanagari unclear: long verse on āma]

Not only the quantum of food in respect of quantity alone cause over-affection of
āma but also the use of food and drinks which are heavy, rough, cold, dry, disliked,
distending, burning, unclean, antagonistic and taken untimely and also while afflicted
with psychic emotions such as passion, anger, greed, confusion, envy, bashfulness,
grief, suffering, excitement and fear. These also cause over-affection of āma. [8]

भवति चात्र–

Here is the verse–

[Devanagari unclear: verse on āma even from wholesome food]

Even the wholesome food also taken in proper quantity, does not get digested
due to anxiety, grief, fear, anger, uncomfortable bed and vigil. [9]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on viṣūcikā and alasaka]

Physicians say two types of the over-affection of āma–viṣūcikā and alasaka. [10]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on viṣūcikā]

Viṣūcikā is that in which āma doṣa comes out through upper and lower passages
and symptoms are found as mentioned above. [11]
