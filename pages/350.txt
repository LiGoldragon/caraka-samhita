[Devanagari unclear: verses on alasaka management]

For the patient suffering from alasaka, hot saline water and clarified butter cooked with
emetic drugs and induction of vomiting–all this should be the immediate management.
The patient is administered enema, suppositories, pasting, fomentation according to
necessity. [13]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on āmaviṣa]

If a person emaciated due to over-saturation eats heavy food, the same becomes
poison instead of food and is then known as āmaviṣa.

[Devanagari unclear: verses on āmaviṣa]

The vitiated āmadoṣa, when there is conflict in therapy, becomes poisonous im-
mediately and gives rise to many disorders, in incurable cases the patient suffers
death. As a result, none should be administered emesis without observing whether
āma is properly digested or not. Cause of pain is removed by reducing measure (light
diet etc.) [14]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on management]

After fasting, food should be given to the patient who is purified by reducing
therapy. The patient suffering from over-saturation having had improperly digested
mass of food but who is strong should be subjected to reducing therapy with foment-
ation, fasting and warm water (after vomiting and purgation). Strong vāmaka (emetic)
or virecana (purgation), if not effective in alleviation of disease, should be tried
with hot, sharp, pungent and rough drugs. After the previous meal is digested, the
patient should take food properly examining suitability and unsuitability. [15-16]
