Here are the verses–

[Devanagari unclear: verses on swelling characteristics]

In vatika swelling, the body parts have swelling, they seem as numbed, are painful
and after pressure regain the usual position. The swelling which is reddish in colour
subsides in night and also by unctuous and hot massage is of vāta type.

In paittika swelling, the patient suffers from thirst and fever, the part has burning
sensation, perspiration, moisture and smell. It starts with the middle of the body, the
patient has yellow eyes, face and skin, thin skin and diarrhoea.

Cold, immobile, itching, pale swelling which does not rebound after pressure is
of kaphaja type. The swelling which does not bleed on cutting with sharp weapon or
kuśa grass but oozes hardly some slimy discharge is also of kaphaja type. Where there
is combination of causes and symptoms of two doṣas, it is dwidoṣaja swelling. The
sānnipātaja has the combination of causes and symptoms of the three doṣas. [9-15]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on swelling locations]

The swelling which starts from feet and spreads all over the body is curable hardly
and also the one which starts from the face in women. In men or women, if the swell-
ing arises from the genitals, it is mostly difficult to be cured and also the one having
complications. [16-17]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on complications]

Vomiting, dyspnoea, anorexia, thirst, fever, diarrhoea and debility–these seven
are the complications of swelling. [8]
