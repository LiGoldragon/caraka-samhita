[Devanagari unclear: verse on pratyakṣa]

Pratyakṣa–Pratyakṣa (perception) is the knowledge which is directly received
by the self and the sense organs. Self-perceived are pleasure, pain, desire, aversion
etc., while sound etc. are percieved by the sense organs. [39]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Anumāna]

Anumāna–Anumāna (inference) is the reasoning supported by invariable
concomittance such as the knowledge of agni by the power of digestion, that of strength
by the power of exercise, auditory organ etc. by the perception of sound etc. [40]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Aitihya]

Aitihya–Aitihya (tradition) is the traditional authoritative source of knowledge
such as veda etc. [41]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Aupamya]

Aupamya–Aupamya (analogy) is the statement of similarity between things such
as–analogy of daṇḍaka with daṇḍa (staff), that of dhanuṣṭhmbha with bow and that
of the provider of health with the archer. [42]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Saṃśaya]

Saṃśaya–Saṃśaya (doubt) is the state of indecision about the concerned entity.
For instance, on observing that both types of persons having or not the signs of
longevity and adopting or not the therapeutic measures–die early and live long, doubt
arises as whether there is untimely death or not. [43]
