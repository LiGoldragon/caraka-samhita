[Devanagari unclear: verse on Kārya]

Kārya (act) is that with the object of performing which the doer proceeds. [72]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Kāryaphala]

Kāryaphala (result of act) is that with the object of which the action is perfor-
med. [73]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Anubandha]

Anubandha (after-effect) is that which essentially enjoins the doer as after-ef-
fect, good or bad, of the action. [74]

देशस्त्वधिष्ठानम् ॥७५॥

Deśa (place) is location. [75]

कालः: पुनः: परिणामः ॥७६॥

Kāla (time) is transformation. [76]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Pravṛtti]

Pravṛtti (inclination) is the effort for the action. This is also known as kriyā,
karma, yatna, karyāsamārambha. [77]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Upāya]

Upāya (procedure) is excellence of the first three factors, kāraṇa etc., and their
proper management leaving aside the kārya, kāryaphala and anubandha. Procedure
leads to performance of an action and thus it is said as upāya. After the action is
performed there is no function of procedure nor is it at the time of performance; after
action is the result and thereafter consequence (so after action also procedure has no
role). [78]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on cautious in actions]

These ten entities should be examined first and thereafter one should proceed for
action. Hence the physician desirous of taking up action should before-hand examine
the entire situation by proper methods and then initiate the action. [79]
