VIII]                         INDRIYASTHĀNA                                  513

[Devanagari unclear: verse on matted eyelashes]

A patient having matted eyelashes and obstructed vision should not be prescribed
medicines.

A patient whose swollen lids do not meet together, who is consumptive and
having oiliness in eyes is like a ghost.

If there are many clear lines and whirls in eyebrows and head without precedence
and apparent cause it indicates death.

On appearance of this sign, the patients survive only for three days while the
healthy persons utmost for six days. [4-7]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on coarseness]

If a healthy or a diseased person does not feel the sensation on pulling and up-
rooting of hairs, he does not survive beyond six days.

A patient whose hairs though un-oiled seem to be oiled, should be taken as hav-
ing obstructed life-span and as such be discarded. [8-9]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on red-flowered nose]

A patient is reducing but his nasal ridge becomes large and though not swol-
len looks as swollen should be discarded by the wise.

If the nostrils are too much dilated or too much constricted and the nose is crooked
or dry–it indicates death.

Because of abnormality if face, ears and lips are white, blackish and excessively
red respectively or the lips are blue (cyanotic), the patient does not recover from
illness. [10-12]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on tongue]

If the patient has slow respiration, coldness of mouth, and tongue rough,
black and unctuous; or, on the contrary, hot and dry; or excessive bleeding from
mouth, with offensive smell or reddish swelling, hard, constipated, dropping or sus-
pended, dies. He should not be treated.

A patient with cracking sound from joints, hardness of bowels with thready
pulse and excessive yawning, dies of pittala-jīrṇa or muṣṭhighra disorders. [16-17]
