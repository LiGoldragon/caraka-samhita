430                           CARAKA-SAṂHITĀ                             [CH

Thus endowed with all the qualities while taking the form of embryo, during
the first month it is completely mixed up and made turbid with all the constituents
and like phlegm having unmanifest form and body parts both as manifest and un-
manifest. [9]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on second month]

During the second month, it is solidified as small bolus, elongated muscle or
tumour-like which indicate male, female and eunuch respectively. [10]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on third month]

During the third month, all the body parts are formed simultaneously. [11]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on body parts as five mahābhūtas]

Some of the body parts have already been said earlier classified as maternal etc.
Now these will be mentioned alongwith some other entities from the point of view of
the products of the mahābhūtas. In fact, the maternal etc. are also the products of the
mahābhūtas. Those derived from ākāśa are sound, the auditory organ, lightness, mi-
nuteness, distinction. Touch, the tactile organ, roughness, impulsion, shaping of dhātus
and physical activities are derived from vāyu. Those derived from tejas are vision, the
visual organ, light, digestion and heat; those derived from ap are taste, the gustatory
organ, coldness, softness, unction and moistening; those derived from pṛthivī are smell,
olfactory organ, heaviness, stability and mass. [12]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on universe and person]

Thus the person is equal to the universe. Whatever formed entities are found in
the universe, they are also found in the person and vice versa. The learned people
want to see the phenomena with this angle. [13]
