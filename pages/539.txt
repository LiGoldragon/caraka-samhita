[Devanagari unclear: verse on smell]

One who perceives the goodness and badness of smell contrarily or does not
percieve the smell entirely, he should be known as devoid of life. [21]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on taste]

If on the rasanā (tongue) inflammation in mouth, if one does not perceive the taste
sensation or perceives it with perversion, he is taken as ripened (for death) by the
experts. [22]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on touch sensations]

If one perceives the touch sensations like hot-cold, coarse-smooth and soft-hard
contrarily, he is about to die. [23]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on supersensory perception]

Super-sensory perception without sustere penance or methodical concentration
of mind indicates death.

If one perceives the senses rightly inspite of incapability of the sense organs, it
indicates death. [24-25]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on contrariety]

If the healthy persons due to contrariety of intellect perceive the senses frequently
in abnormal form though they are not so, they should be considered as almost
dead. [26]

तत्र श्लोकः–

Now the summing up verse–

[Devanagari unclear: summing verse]

The physician who is possessed of the correct knowledge about the senses, is
able to know the life and death. [27]
