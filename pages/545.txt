508                           CARAKA-SAṂHITĀ                             [CH

If a patient of abdominal disease passes urine abnormally-viscous and faeces as
scabulous having lowered temperature and dyspnoea does not survive.

If one suffers from oedema which starting from abdomen spreads to the ex-
tremities, he is killed by the disease after putting the family members in great calamity.

One who has oedema in feet, calf muscles laxed and both the legs weakened
should be discarded by physician.

A physician should not prescribe medicine for a patient who suffers from oedema
in hand, feet, private parts and abdomen alongwith reduced complexion, strength and
food. [11-14]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on expectorants]

The patient who expectorates profuse sputum having blue or yellow colour or
with blood should be discarded even from distance.

The learned physician should discard from distance a patient having horripilation,
viscous urine, oedema, cough, fever and emaciation.

If a patient who is emaciated and weak gets afflicted with distressing aggrava-
tion of all the three doṣas, should not be treated.

Fever and diarrhoea at the end of oedema and oedema at the end of the former
two particularly in a weak patient indicates death.

The patient who is aneamic, extremely emaciated, and has excessive thirst, ab-
dominal distension and difficult respiration should be discarded by the learned physi-
cian. (15-19)

[Devanagari unclear: verse on rigidity]

Rigidity in jaws and carotid region, thirst, excessive reduction of strength and
vital breath confined only to the chest indicate the patient's death. [20]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on death]

If a patient gets fainted with voilent movements of the body without having any
relief and has diminished flesh, strength and diet, he is to die soon.
