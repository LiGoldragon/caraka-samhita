If repelling the forehands (of attendants) one revolves his head with distress,
and has profuse sweating on forehead and looseness of joints he is about to die. [21-26]

तत्र श्लोकः–

[Devanagari unclear: summing verse]

Now the summing up verse–

The wise physician should observe attentively these signs in persons who are
about to die because some of them after arising disappear in a moment and no sign
goes without its consequence. [27]

CHAPTER IX

अथातो यस्य श्यावनिमित्तीयमिन्द्रियं व्याख्यास्यामः ॥१॥

Now (I) shall expound the chapter on blackish sign etc. [1]

इति ह स्माह भगवानात्रेयः ॥२॥

As propounded by Lord Ātreya. [2]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on blackish patient]

The patient whose body has blackish complexion, dryness of mouth-throat-nose,
weakness of voice, voice with cracking, dry, low and slow tone, and is groaning
loudly or is laughing inopportunely is about to die.
