[Devanagari unclear: continuing on water properties]

Cold water alleviates fainting, fatigue, exhaustion, heat, alcoholic complica-
tions, vomiting, internal haemorrhage, biliousness, bleeding, narcosis and pittaja
disorders.

Hot water is appetising, agni-stimulating, helpful in digestion, alleviates ho-
arseness of voice, alleviates kapha, vāta and constipation. Hot water reduced to one-
fourth on boiling is even more potent.

Water from various sources differs in properties according to soil, location, time
and season. Water from cold mountains is wholesome and digestive, while that from
marshy areas may be heavy and unwholesome. Rain water is naturally pure and re-
freshing. Water collected during the autumn (haṃsodaka) is considered the best.

The water of various seasons should be considered: in autumn, the water is purest;
in late autumn it is slightly heavy; in winter it is unctuous; in spring it is bitter; in sum-
mer it produces vāta but is most beneficial; in rainy season the water from new wells
should be avoided.

Drinking water in moderate quantity at proper times is very beneficial. Excess
water at improper times can produce indigestion, heart disease and cause aggrava-
tion of doṣas.

Special water preparations:
- Boiled water cooled with herbs has special properties
- Medicated waters (with specific herbs) are therapeutic
- Water from young coconut is sweet, cooling and saturating
- Cane juice is sweet, nutritive, and alleviates pitta and vāta
