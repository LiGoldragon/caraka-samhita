I]                            NIDĀNASTHĀNA                                  253

Thus the subject matter of the nidānasthāna has been mentioned in brief. The
same will be dealt with in details further. [14]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on eight diseases]

First of all, (I) will describe, in the order beginning with etiology, the earliest
eight diseases arisen from greed, malice and anger, alongwith are principles of
therapeutics in brief. Later on in the section on therapeutics (I) will describe the later
developed disorders. [15]

[Devanagari unclear: verses on first to deal with somatic disease]

Amongst the diseases, fever is described first because of its being the earliest
(in appearance) of the somatic diseases. [16]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on eight types of fever]

Now listen–the causative factors of fever; soyes are eight in number such as–vāta,
pitta, kapha, vāta-pitta, vāta-kapha, pitta-kapha, vāta-pitta-kapha and the eighth as
exogenous. [17]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on diagnosis]

Further (I) will describe the etiology, prodroma, symptoms and therapeutic suit-
ability of fever. [18]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on vāta-fever]

Vāyu gets vitiated by over-use of rough, light, cold (substances), excessive ad-
ministration of emesis, purgation, non-unctuous enema and head exacuation, physical
exercise, suppression of natural urges, fasting, fear, injury, sexual intercourse, excitement,
anxiety, excessive blood-letting, vigils and uneven postures of body. [19]

[Devanagari unclear: long verses on vātika fever]

When this vitiated (vāta) enters into āmāśaya and being mixed up with heat,
accompanying the initial dhātu produced by digestion of food and known as
'Rasa', blocking the channels carrying rasa and sweda (sweat) and affecting the agni
takes out the heat from the seat of digestion and spreads it all over the body, it
produces fever. [20]
