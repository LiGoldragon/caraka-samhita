386                           CARAKA-SAṂHITĀ                             [CH

The therapy administered after or before the (opportune) time is not effective because
time determines the sufficiency of the administration of therapy. [128]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on initiation of therapy]

Pravṛtti (inclination) is the initiation of therapy. It is characterised by the combi-
nation of physician, drug, patient and attendant in treatment. [129]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on upāya]

Upāya (procedure) is excellence of physician etc. and their proper arrangment. It
is characterized by physicians etc. endowed with their respective qualities and proper
application of the therapy alongwith (the consideration) of place, time, dose, suitabil-
ity, processing etc. which are the factors leading to success. [130]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on ten entities]

Thus these ten entities should be examined separately. [131]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on Pratipatti]

The object of examination is 'Pratipatti' (to decide the course of action). 'Pratipatti'
is the knowledge of treatment with which the disorder is to be afflicted. [132]

[Devanagari unclear: verse on indications]

The condition in which emesis etc. are indicated or contra-indicated will be de-
scribed in detail in siddhi section later on. [133]

[Devanagari unclear: long verse on combined situations]

In case where symptoms of both indication and contra-indication are conjoined
one should decide in either way on basis of predominance or otherwise. The diseases
are mentioned in treatises in respect of their treatment as general rule or exception.
Hence it is said that one should take action after considering the predominance or
otherwise. [134]
