# Sūtrasthāna 27 — the fruit verses, verified and renumbered against GRETIL

## Why this note

The downstream articles in [*The Book of Sol*](../../../TheBookOfSol/) — chiefly [*Yogis Don't Eat Fruit*](../../../TheBookOfSol/diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md) and the [`Fruit_as_Medicine.md`](../../../TheBookOfSol/source-extracts/Fruit_as_Medicine.md) extract — lean on a cluster of *Caraka Saṃhitā* Sūtrasthāna 27 verses classifying individual fruits (grapes, mango, *bibhītaka*, *āmalaka*, pomegranate, the six *tridoṣaghna* fruits).

A reader doubting the edition asked, in effect: *is the version we have valid?* Checking the cited verses against GRETIL's authoritative Unicode Sanskrit (Oliver Hellwig's contribution, which covers Sūtrasthāna 1, 12, 26, 27, 28 — the food chapters included) gives a clear two-part answer:

1. **The content is authentic.** Every fruit verse the project quotes is present, verbatim in substance, in the GRETIL Sanskrit. The translations are faithful. The text is not corrupt.
2. **The digest's verse *numbers* were wrong.** The `Fruit_as_Medicine` extract had assigned per-fruit numbers (grapes "27.143", mango "27.146", *āmalaka* "27.156", and so on) that match neither GRETIL nor the project's own Sharma-2014 chapter markdown. These were extraction errors, now corrected to the GRETIL/Trikamji numbers below.

This is **not** an edition-level renumbering. It is a localized mistake in one digest, and it is fixed.

## How we know the old numbers were wrong

Three independent checks converge:

- **The digest contradicted itself.** Its own section intro states that "the fruit-group runs from verse 27.125 to 27.165," and that grapes (*drākṣā*) is the *first* fruit of the group — yet it numbered grapes 27.143. A first fruit in a group beginning at 125 cannot be verse 143.
- **GRETIL places grapes at the start.** GRETIL marks 27.124 as the *śāka-varga* (vegetable-group) closing verse — *caturthaḥ śākavargo 'yaṃ pattrakandaphalāśrayaḥ* — and 27.125–126 as grapes: *mṛdvīkā bṛṃhaṇī vṛṣyā madhurā snigdhaśītalā*. Grapes is the first fruit, verse 125.
- **Sharma agrees with GRETIL.** The project's own Sharma-2014 markdown ([`../../sharma-2014/01-sutrasthana/27-annapanavidhi.md`](../../sharma-2014/01-sutrasthana/27-annapanavidhi.md)) opens its *phala-varga* (अथ फलवर्गः) with grapes, then *kharjūra*, *paruṣaka/madhūka*, *āmrāta*, *tāla/nārikela*, *bhavya* — the **same order as GRETIL**. There is no large offset between the editions; the fruit catalogue is structured identically. The digest's numbers simply did not follow it.

## Corrected verse mapping (GRETIL / Trikamji)

Each verse below is the authoritative GRETIL Sanskrit (markers `Car_1,27.NNN`), with the wrong number the digest formerly carried.

### Grapes (*mṛdvīkā* / *drākṣā*) — GRETIL 27.125–126 (digest had "27.143")

> tṛṣṇādāhajvaraśvāsaraktapittakṣatakṣayān /\
> vātapittamudāvartaṃ svarabhedaṃ madātyayam //\
> tiktāsyatām āsyaśoṣaṃ kāsaṃ cāśu vyapohati /\
> **mṛdvīkā** bṛṃhaṇī vṛṣyā madhurā snigdhaśītalā // *Car_1,27.125–126*

### Mango (*āmra*), three forms — GRETIL 27.139 (digest had "27.146")

> raktapittakaraṃ bālam āpūrṇaṃ pittavardhanam /\
> pakvam **āmraṃ** jayed vāyuṃ māṃsaśukrabalapradam // *Car_1,27.139*

### Six *tridoṣaghna* / *viṣaghna* fruits — GRETIL 27.145–146 (digest had "27.158")

> nīpaṃ śatāhvakaṃ pīlu **tṛṇaśūnyaṃ** vikaṅkatam /\
> prācīnāmalakaṃ caiva doṣaghnaṃ garahāri ca // *Car_1,27.145–146*

### *Āmalaka*, all rasas except saline — GRETIL 27.147 (digest had "27.156")

> tindukaṃ kaphapittaghnaṃ kaṣāyaṃ madhuraṃ laghu /\
> vidyād **āmalake** sarvān rasāṃl lavaṇavarjitān // *Car_1,27.147*

### *Bibhītaka*, by tissue and disease — GRETIL 27.148 (digest had "27.158b")

> rūkṣaṃ svādu kaṣāyaṃ kaphapittaharaṃ param /\
> rasāsṛṅmāṃsamedojān doṣān hanti **vibhītakam** // *Car_1,27.148*

### Pomegranate (*dāḍima*), three varieties — GRETIL 27.149–150 (digest had "27.157")

> amlaṃ kaṣāyamadhuraṃ vātaghnaṃ grāhi dīpanam /\
> snigdhoṣṇaṃ **dāḍimaṃ** hṛdyaṃ kaphapittavirodhi ca /\
> rūkṣāmlaṃ dāḍimaṃ yattu tatpittānilakopanam /\
> madhuraṃ pittanut teṣāṃ pūrvaṃ dāḍimam uttamam // *Car_1,27.149–150*

The rasa-overview verse the digest cites as 27.4 (*svāduramlādibhir yogaṃ...*, the taste-by-doṣa rule) is correct as given — GRETIL also numbers it 27.4. The error was confined to the per-fruit run.

## The fourth fruit, resolved: *tṛṇaśūnya* = *ketakī* (Cakrapāṇi)

One apparent textual divergence remained: the six-fruit verse's fourth item reads *tṛṇaśūnya* in GRETIL but *ketakī* in the digest. Cakrapāṇidatta's *Āyurveda Dīpikā* settles it. In the Harinātha Viśārada edition of Caraka-with-commentary — on disk at `library/sa/ayurveda/caraka-cakrapani-harinatha.zip` — the root reads *tṛṇaśūnyaṃ*, and the commentary glosses the verse's fruit-names one by one:

> nīpaṃ kadambakam, śatāhvaka-phalaṃ śarakā iti khyātam, pīlu auttarāpathikam, **tṛṇaśūnyam ketakī-phalam**, prācīnāmalakaṃ pānīyāmalakam.
>
> "nīpa is the kadamba; the śatāhva-fruit is known as *śarakā*; pīlu is the northern kind; **tṛṇaśūnya is the ketakī-fruit**; prācīnāmalaka is the water-āmalaka."\
> — Cakrapāṇidatta, *Āyurveda Dīpikā* on the six-fruit verse (Devanagari per the Harinātha OCR, normalised to IAST)

The two name one fruit. The critical text prints the original word, *tṛṇaśūnya*; a translator may print the commentary's identification of it, *ketakī*. The *doṣaghna* / *viṣaghna* claim stands unchanged.

(One curiosity worth recording: the Harinātha edition numbers this verse **124**, against GRETIL's 145–146. That *is* a genuine edition-numbering difference — but it is a difference between two *Sanskrit* editions, and does not license the digest's earlier "158": that number matched no edition. The same Cakrapāṇi page also quotes *Suśruta* on the adjacent pomegranate verse — *the sweet pomegranate removes all three doṣas, the sour subdues vāta and kapha* — the Caraka–Suśruta concord attested inside the commentary.)

## Guidance for downstream citations

- The **substance** of the Sū. 27 fruit citations is verified and may be relied on.
- Cite the fruit verses by the **GRETIL / Trikamji numbers above** — which is the numbering the digest's own stated Sanskrit base (the Trikamji critical edition) implies, and which [`Fruit_as_Medicine.md`](../../../TheBookOfSol/source-extracts/Fruit_as_Medicine.md) now carries. The earlier per-fruit numbers were wrong and have been replaced.
- This is a *different* problem from the one documented for Sū. 5.12 in [`sutrasthana-5-12.md`](./sutrasthana-5-12.md). There the issue is Sharma's *interpretive English* (the *saindhavān* and *dhānyaṃ* readings); here it was simply a wrong number in a digest. Neither casts doubt on the Sanskrit text itself.

## What remains

1. **Extend the GRETIL cross-numbering** to the rest of the Sū. 27 fruit and vegetable catalogue (27.88–165). The per-item GRETIL anchors are folded into [`../fruit-vegetable-warnings.md`](../fruit-vegetable-warnings.md) as of this pass.
2. **Cakrapāṇidatta's *Āyurveda Dīpikā* is on disk** (the Harinātha edition; recoverable for targeted lookups, see [`../translation-sources.md`](./translation-sources.md)). The Sharma/Dash 7-volume set remains worth acquiring for clean publication-grade commentary text.
3. GRETIL does not cover Sū. 5 or Sū. 6, so the daily-routine verse (5.12) and the seasonal-regimen chapter (6, *Tasyāśitīya*) cannot be GRETIL-verified; those rest on Sharma plus the wisdomlib cross-check.
