# Sūtrasthāna 27 — the fruit verses verified against GRETIL

## Why this note

The downstream articles in [*The Book of Sol*](../../../TheBookOfSol/) — chiefly [*Yogis Don't Eat Fruit*](../../../TheBookOfSol/diet/Yogis_Dont_Eat_Fruit.md) and the [`Fruit_as_Medicine.md`](../../../TheBookOfSol/source-extracts/Fruit_as_Medicine.md) extract — lean on a cluster of *Caraka Saṃhitā* Sūtrasthāna 27 verses classifying individual fruits (grapes, mango, *āmalaka*, pomegranate, the six *tridoṣaghna* fruits). Those citations carry Priya Vrat Sharma's verse numbers (27.143, 27.146, 27.156, 27.157, 27.158).

A reader doubting the edition asked, in effect: *is the version we have valid?* This note answers the question for the fruit chapter by checking the project's quotes against an independent, authoritative source — the GRETIL (Göttingen) Unicode Sanskrit text, which covers Sūtrasthāna 1, 12, 26, 27, and 28. Chapter 27 is fully present.

The result is two-sided and worth stating plainly:

1. **The content is sound.** Every fruit verse the project cites is found, verbatim in substance, in the GRETIL Sanskrit. The translations are faithful. The project is not quoting a corrupt or invented text.
2. **The numbering diverges.** The verse *numbers* in the Sharma 2014 edition (which the project's citations follow) do not match GRETIL's numbering — and not by a constant offset. A bare citation like "Caraka Sū. 27.146" is therefore unambiguous only when the edition is named.

## The finding: content verified, numbering divergent

GRETIL's chapter 27 fruit group runs from verse 27.125. The project's Sharma-numbered citations map onto it as follows. The GRETIL Sanskrit is quoted from the file `caraka_u.htm` (gretil.sub.uni-goettingen.de, `1_sanskr/6_sastra/7_ayur/`), markers `Car_1,27.NNN`.

### Grapes — project "Sū. 27.143" = GRETIL 27.125–126

> tṛṣṇādāhajvaraśvāsaraktapittakṣatakṣayān /\
> vātapittamudāvartaṃ svarabhedaṃ madātyayam //\
> tiktāsyatām āsyaśoṣaṃ kāsaṃ cāśu vyapohati /\
> **mṛdvīkā** bṛṃhaṇī vṛṣyā madhurā snigdhaśītalā // *Car_1,27.125–126*

*mṛdvīkā* (grapes) — alleviates thirst, burning, fever, dyspnoea, *raktapitta*, wounds, wasting, *vāta-pitta*, *udāvarta*, hoarseness, alcoholism, bitterness and dryness of mouth, cough; bulk-promoting, aphrodisiac, sweet, unctuous, cold. This is the project's "grapes alleviate thirst, burning sensation, fever..." (Fruit_as_Medicine §II, cited as Sū. 27.143). Content: exact. Number: GRETIL 27.125–126, not 143.

### Mango — project "Sū. 27.146" = GRETIL 27.139

> raktapittakaraṃ bālam āpūrṇaṃ pittavardhanam /\
> pakvam **āmraṃ** jayed vāyuṃ māṃsaśukrabalapradam // *Car_1,27.139*

The young (*bāla*) causes *raktapitta*; the full/mature increases *pitta*; the ripe (*pakva*) mango conquers *vāyu* and promotes flesh, semen, strength. This is the project's "the young fruits of āmra cause internal haemorrhage, the mature ones aggravate pitta, the ripe ones alleviate vāyu..." (cited as Sū. 27.146). Content: exact. Number: GRETIL 27.139.

### Āmalaka — project "Sū. 27.156" = GRETIL 27.147

> tindukaṃ kaphapittaghnaṃ kaṣāyaṃ madhuraṃ laghu /\
> vidyād **āmalake** sarvān rasāṃl lavaṇavarjitān // *Car_1,27.147*

"Know in *āmalaka* all the *rasas* except *lavaṇa* (saline)." The project's "in āmalaka there are all the rasas except lavaṇa" (cited as Sū. 27.156). Content: exact. Number: GRETIL 27.147.

### Pomegranate — project "Sū. 27.157" = GRETIL 27.149–150

> amlaṃ kaṣāyamadhuraṃ vātaghnaṃ grāhi dīpanam /\
> snigdhoṣṇaṃ **dāḍimaṃ** hṛdyaṃ kaphapittavirodhi ca /\
> rūkṣāmlaṃ dāḍimaṃ yattu tatpittānilakopanam /\
> madhuraṃ pittanut teṣāṃ pūrvaṃ dāḍimam uttamam // *Car_1,27.149–150*

Three varieties: sour-astringent-sweet (best, *vāta*-alleviating, non-conflicting with *kapha-pitta*); only-sour-rough (aggravates *pitta-vāta*); only-sweet (alleviates *pitta*); the first is best. The project's three-variety pomegranate passage (cited as Sū. 27.157). Content: exact. Number: GRETIL 27.149–150.

### The six *tridoṣaghna* / *viṣaghna* fruits — project "Sū. 27.158" = GRETIL 27.145–146

> nīpaṃ śatāhvakaṃ pīlu **tṛṇaśūnyaṃ** vikaṅkatam /\
> prācīnāmalakaṃ caiva doṣaghnaṃ garahāri ca // *Car_1,27.145–146*

Six fruits "*doṣaghna* (doṣa-destroying) and *garahāri* (poison-removing)." The project lists these as *"nīpa, śatāhvaka, pīlu, **ketakī**, vikaṅkata, prācīnāmalaka"* (cited as Sū. 27.158). Content: matches — **except a variant reading**: where Sharma/the project has *ketakī*, GRETIL reads *tṛṇaśūnya*. See below.

## The fourth fruit, resolved: *tṛṇaśūnya* = *ketakī* (Cakrapāṇi)

The fourth fruit first looked like a genuine textual divergence rather than a numbering one: Sharma's text (and so the project) reads *ketakī*, while GRETIL's Trikamji-based root text reads *tṛṇaśūnya*. Cakrapāṇidatta's *Āyurveda Dīpikā* settles it. In the Harinātha Viśārada edition of Caraka-with-commentary — on disk at `library/sa/ayurveda/caraka-cakrapani-harinatha.zip` — the root reads *tṛṇaśūnyaṃ*, and the commentary glosses the verse's fruit-names one by one:

> nīpaṃ kadambakam, śatāhvaka-phalaṃ śarakā iti khyātam, pīlu auttarāpathikam, **tṛṇaśūnyam ketakī-phalam**, prācīnāmalakaṃ pānīyāmalakam.
>
> "nīpa is the kadamba; the śatāhva-fruit is known as *śarakā*; pīlu is the northern kind; **tṛṇaśūnya is the ketakī-fruit**; prācīnāmalaka is the water-āmalaka."\
> — Cakrapāṇidatta, *Āyurveda Dīpikā* on the six-fruit verse (Devanagari per the Harinātha OCR, normalised to IAST)

The two name one fruit. The critical text prints the original word, *tṛṇaśūnya*; Sharma prints the commentary's identification of it, *ketakī*. The divergence dissolves, and the *doṣaghna* / *viṣaghna* claim stands unchanged across all three editions.

Two notes from the same page. The Harinātha edition numbers this verse **124** — a third numbering, against Sharma's 158 and GRETIL's 145–146 — which only sharpens the lesson that Caraka verse-numbers are edition-specific while the content is not. And Cakrapāṇi, glossing the adjacent pomegranate verse, quotes *Suśruta* by name (the pomegranate is twofold, sweet and sour; the sweet removes all three doṣas, the sour subdues vāta and kapha) — the Caraka–Suśruta concord attested inside the commentary itself.

## Consequence for downstream citations

The numbering offsets are not constant — grapes shift by ~18 verses (143→125), mango by ~7 (146→139), *āmalaka* by ~9 (156→147). A single additive correction will not reconcile the two editions; the divergence is structural, arising from how the two editions split and count the prose-and-verse fruit catalogue.

Practical rule for *The Book of Sol* and any other consumer:

- The **substance** of the Sū. 27 fruit citations is verified and may be relied on.
- The **verse number** must travel with its edition: write "Caraka Sū. 27.146 (Sharma 2014)" or cite the GRETIL number "Sū. 27.139 (Trikamji/GRETIL)" — never a bare "27.146" as if edition-neutral.
- Where a downstream article quotes one of these verses, the [`Fruit_as_Medicine.md`](../../../TheBookOfSol/source-extracts/Fruit_as_Medicine.md) extract already names the edition (Sharma 2014); this note supplies the GRETIL cross-number for verification.

This is the same discipline applied to Sū. 5.12 in [`sutrasthana-5-12.md`](./sutrasthana-5-12.md) — there the problem was Sharma's *interpretive English* (the *saindhavān* and *dhānyaṃ* readings); here the problem is *edition-specific numbering*. In both cases the corrective is the same: name the edition, cross-check against the critical Sanskrit, and treat the bare verse number as provisional.

## What remains

1. **Cakrapāṇidatta's *Āyurveda Dīpikā* is on disk after all.** The Harinātha Viśārada edition (`library/sa/ayurveda/caraka-cakrapani-harinatha.zip`) carries the commentary, and its OCR is recoverable for targeted lookups — it adjudicated the *tṛṇaśūnya* / *ketakī* fruit above. The 1922 Nirṇaya-Sāgara scan rejected as unrecoverable is a *different* edition; the Harinātha scan is usable. The Sharma/Dash 7-volume printed set is still worth acquiring for clean, publication-grade commentary text, but verse-level commentary questions can now be answered from the Harinātha OCR — see [`translation-sources.md`](../translation-sources.md).
2. **Extend the GRETIL cross-numbering** to the rest of the Sū. 27 fruit and vegetable catalogue (27.125–165) and fold the GRETIL numbers into [`../fruit-vegetable-warnings.md`](../fruit-vegetable-warnings.md) as a parallel column.
3. **Note** that GRETIL does not cover Sū. 5 or Sū. 6, so the daily-routine verse (5.12) and the seasonal-regimen chapter (6, *Tasyāśitīya*) cannot be GRETIL-verified; those rest on Sharma plus the wisdomlib cross-check.
